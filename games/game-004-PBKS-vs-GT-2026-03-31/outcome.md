**What we got RIGHT:**
- PP was indeed close (54 vs 55 — within 1 run)
- Jansen DID get a key wicket (Sudharsan for 13 in over 3.4)
- Both PPs were aggressive as predicted
- Prabhsimran scored well (37 off 24)

**What we got WRONG:**
- Arya failed (7 off 8, dismissed by Rabada in over 2). We had him as the explosive threat.
- Gill didn't face much of Arshdeep in the way we modeled (Arshdeep's PP wasn't dominant)
- Rabada bowling in PP (we predicted he wouldn't play)

**PP Scenario that actually played out:** Closest to **Scenario B** (PBKS PP edge via Jansen striking) for GT's innings, but **Scenario D** elements for PBKS (Arya fell early). Net was Scenario C (even PP). **Our assessment was correct — PP was indeed roughly even.**

**Actual (PBKS batting in middle):** PBKS went from 55/1 to 118/6 by over ~15 = ~63 runs, 5 wickets. **MASSIVE COLLAPSE: 4 wickets for 8 runs (overs 12-15).** Prasidh Krishna destroyed PBKS middle order (Iyer 18, Shashank 4, Stoinis DUCK).

**What we got RIGHT:**

- Rashid was better than 2025 (eco 7.25 vs 9.30 season avg) but not vintage (1 wicket). We gave 20% to "Rashid vintage spell" — the actual outcome was between our Scenario B (vintage) and C (both effective). Reasonable.

**What we got WRONG:**

- But we completely missed the **PBKS batting collapse** in middle overs. 4 wickets for 8 runs. Prasidh (not Rashid) was the destroyer. We focused on Rashid vs Iyer but the actual damage came from Prasidh's pace.
- Connolly, who we flagged as "high risk vs leg-spin," was actually the one who survived and scored 72*. He handled Rashid and everyone else.

**Middle-overs verdict:** Our BOWLING prediction was excellent (PBKS restricted GT effectively). Our BATTING prediction missed the Prasidh-driven collapse entirely.

## Prediction Review

### What the prediction got RIGHT:

- **Chahal at Mullanpur is now a proven pattern.** 4-0-28-2 in Game 004, following 4/28 vs KKR in 2025. Two near-identical performances at this venue. Chahal at Mullanpur should be treated as a base-rate fact in future predictions.
- **First innings score was close.** 162 vs predicted 165-180. Slightly below range but within the broader 155-195 window.
- **Powerplay was correctly assessed as even.** GT 54/1, PBKS 55/1 — within 1 run of each other.
- **Gill as GT top scorer.** Predicted 35-55 at SR 140-160. Actual: 39(27) at 144. Nearly perfect.
- **Rashid's form was between 2025 decline and career level.** Eco 7.25 (better than 9.30 season avg, worse than ~6.5 career). Our framing of this as the "swing variable" was correct — he was better than feared but not vintage.
- **Jansen was effective early.** Got Sudharsan in over 3.4 (we predicted this left-arm angle vs LHB was dangerous).
- **PBKS batting depth eventually won the game.** Connolly + Bartlett at 7-8 rescued from 118/6. We correctly identified batting depth as a PBKS advantage.

### What the prediction got WRONG:

- **Winner.** GT 55% / PBKS 45%. PBKS won. We were on the wrong side.
- **Holder not playing was the critical miss.** Our entire +5% death-overs edge (the largest single adjustment) was built on Holder's death bowling. He wasn't selected. Rabada played instead. This is an XI prediction failure, not a matchup analysis failure.
- **Toss prediction wrong.** We predicted bat first (55% venue edge). PBKS won and chose to field. The bat-first advantage didn't hold this game.
- **Arshdeep had his worst game.** 0/42, eco 10.50, 4 wides + 1 NB in the final over. We called him "India's best death bowler" and "world class." He had a terrible night.
- **Vyshak was the star, not the weak link.** 3/34 was PBKS's best bowling performance. We explicitly called Vyshak a "weak link" and "unproven." Wrong.
- **Connolly was the hero, not the risk.** 72*(44) MOTM. We flagged him as "high risk vs leg-spin" and "unproven." He handled Rashid and carried PBKS through a collapse. Our entire framing of Connolly was backwards.
- **Stoinis was supposed to be the death finisher.** He scored a duck. Our death batting scenario centered on Stoinis targeting Prasidh — instead Stoinis fell to Prasidh for 0.
- **Sundar bowled well (eco 6.75).** We said he was a "clear GT weakness" at eco 10.09. He was GT's most economical bowler.

### Single biggest error:

**The Holder selection call.** We gave 0% probability to Holder not playing. Our entire death-overs framework — the largest single phase adjustment at +5% GT — was predicated on Holder being in the XI. When the XI was announced without Holder, our GT edge evaporated instantly. This is the same error type as Game 003 (XI prediction). We need to build XI uncertainty INTO the probability model, not treat our predicted XI as certain.

### Second biggest error:

**Overvaluing reputation, undervaluing unknowns.** Arshdeep (reputation: elite) had his worst game. Vyshak (reputation: weak link) had his best. Connolly (reputation: unproven risk) won MOTM. Stoinis (reputation: strong finisher) scored a duck. When we don't have data, we defaulted to reputation — and reputation was systematically wrong in this game.
