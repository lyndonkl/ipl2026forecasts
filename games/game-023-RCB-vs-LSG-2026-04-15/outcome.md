### What We Got Wrong (1 Specific)

   - Prediction: RCB PP edge compressed to 1.08 due to Shami 2.71 Impact
   - Outcome: RCB 60/1 (10 RPO) — at top of Bullish 1.15 envelope, not compressed
   - **Root Cause:** Kohli's form surge (166.75 L5 SR +139) > Shami's elite economy (+233 Eco+). The impact offset logic assumed symmetric play, but matchup volatility favored batter form on this night.
   - **Magnitude:** If RCB PP LR held 1.15 instead of 1.08, model probability would be ~71% vs locked 68%. Margin: 3pp.

### Single Biggest Error

**RCB PP 1.08 compression was the single largest model miss.** The red-team adjustment was well-intentioned (compress overconfident new-ball edge) but applied the wrong logic (Impact offset instead of matchup volatility). This cost 3pp of probability mass.

**Counterfactual:** If PP LR had remained 1.15, the locked probability would have been ~70-71%, and the Brier score would have been ~0.09 (even better calibration). The locked 68% is still excellent (Brier 0.1024), but the 2-3pp improvement would have been within reach.

### Unforecastable Event

**Kohli's 3-boundary over off Shami (15 runs)** was the single unforecastable event. This was a high-variance matchup with no prior season data. Both players are elite (Kohli SR+ 139, Shami Eco+ 233), and the outcome was driven by luck/execution in a specific 6-ball sequence. This is intrinsic T20 noise, not a process failure.

### Lessons for Future Games

1. **Form surge + elite bowler = high variance.** Don't apply symmetric Impact offsets. Instead, expand confidence interval (e.g., 68% ± 5pp) when two elite players face off with new matchup data.
2. **Context-dependent form ceilings.** Player form (e.g., Samad) is conditional on team batting order, opposition bowling, and match situation. Add filters: "This player's form ceiling applies if [conditions]."
3. **Workload flags ≠ form indicators.** Flag overexposure as a durability risk for *future* games, not as a current form depressor. Rasikh's 11.4 recent Eco was manageable; standard allocation + tail exposure = elite performance.
4. **Dew advantage timing.** If chaser finishes before overs 15, dew is immaterial. Don't overweight +1.10 LR if chase pace suggests early finish. (This was correct in model; RCB's 10 RPO PP meant dew was always going to be marginal.)
