## 7. Brier Score Computation

**Locked Prediction:** CSK 52.2% / KKR 47.8%  
**Actual Winner:** CSK  
**Brier Score Formula:** (P_assigned_to_actual_winner - 1)²

**Calculation:**
- P_assigned = 0.522 (52.2% for CSK)
- Actual = CSK won (outcome = 1)
- Brier = (0.522 - 1)² = (−0.478)² = **0.2285**

**Brier Score: 0.2285**

**Interpretation:**
- Brier of 0.2285 is **below the random baseline of 0.25** (coin flip).

- However, CSK's dominant 32-run victory suggests we were **underconfident** — a probability closer to 60–65% would have been better calibrated.
- **Recalibration note:** CSK's actual dominance (batting 192/5, KKR collapse 160/7) suggests the match was less competitive than our 52.2/47.8 split implied. We assigned near-neutral odds to what was a relatively one-sided match.

1. **Winner prediction:** CSK won (52.2% was correct direction) ✅

4. **CSK batting phases:** PP/EM mostly accurate; Samson opening was adaptive ✅

### What We Missed

1. **Noor Ahmad's individual impact (HIGH IMPACT):** 
   - **Forecastability:** HIGH — Noor Ahmad's form and recent performances should have been flagged in Player Form Profiles.
   - **Evidence:** He took 3/21; was clearly the standout bowler.
   - **Systemic issue:** Player Form Profiles listed CSK's spin "depth" (Chahal + Noor + others) as a general category. Noor Ahmad's individual 3-wicket haul was not anticipated as likely.
   - **Fix:** Future EM-phase analysis should weight left-arm wrist-spinners on turning pitches as potential high-impact individuals, not just part of the "spin unit."

2. **CSK Death-phase underperformance (MEDIUM IMPACT):**
   - **Prediction:** We assigned LR 1.54 (strong CSK edge) based on "KKR pace gutted (Pathirana, Rana OUT)."
   - **Actual:** CSK scored only 25–30 in death (decent, not fortress).
   - **Forecastability:** MEDIUM — dependent on whether Arora/Tyagi actually stepped up. We knew KKR pace was thin but perhaps overestimated their weakness.
   - **Evidence:** Arora took a key CSK wicket (Mhatre) and seemed to bowl tight in death.
   - **Fix:** When a player is missing, look for replacement performance data rather than assuming baseline decline.

3. **KKR EM-phase collapse (HIGH IMPACT):**
   - **Prediction:** Scenario predicted Raghuvanshi (168 SR) would challenge CSK spin (LR 0.833, = CSK edge).
   - **Actual:** Raghuvanshi collapsed vs Noor Ahmad; KKR EM was disastrous.
   - **Forecastability:** HIGH — Noor Ahmad's match-winning form should have been flagged.
   - **Underlying miss:** We did not distinguish **individual bowler impact** vs. **team spin depth**. Noor Ahmad on a turning pitch is not equivalent to "CSK spin depth."

4. **Overall match competitiveness (MEDIUM IMPACT):**
   - **Prediction:** 52.2/47.8 split suggested a close match.
   - **Actual:** 32-run margin suggests a one-sided match.
   - **Forecastability:** MEDIUM — dependent on whether we should have recognized CSK's EM-phase dominance (Noor Ahmad) earlier.
   - **Systemic issue:** Our final Posterior Computation showed Toss-weighted probability of 52.2%, which is very close to 50/50. This suggests we were uncertain between the two scenarios.
   - **Fix:** If both Scenario A and B are within 2–3%, that's a flag for high uncertainty, not neutral odds. Consider flagging this to Kushal as a match with high variance.
