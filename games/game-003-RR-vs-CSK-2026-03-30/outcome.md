## Prediction Review

### What the prediction got right:

- **Direction correct:** We favoured RR and RR won convincingly.
- **Scenario A was the correct scenario:** Our Scenario A (40% — "Debutants struggle, RR openers fire") played out almost exactly. CSK's top order collapsed, debutant-level batsmen couldn't handle RR bowling, Suryavanshi exploded in the chase.
- **Toss prediction correct:** We predicted bowl first and RR did choose to bowl.
- **Leg-spin venue pattern validated:** While Bishnoi wasn't the top wicket-taker, the overall leg-spin/spin narrative held — Jadeja (2/18) was devastating against his old team, confirming spin's importance at Barsapara.
- **CSK depth weakness thesis confirmed:** Post-Dube, CSK had no one to rescue the innings except Overton (who we correctly identified as avg 10.50, SR 101.2 in T20s — he outperformed expectations with 43 off 36 but couldn't save the team).
- **Jaiswal vs Khaleel assessment correct:** We said Jaiswal dominates Khaleel (H2H SR 200+). Jaiswal went unbeaten.

### What the prediction missed or got wrong:

- **First innings score massively overestimated:** Predicted 175–190, actual 127 all out. This is a 48-run miss — the largest error in any sub-prediction across 3 games. The venue average (180) was irrelevant when CSK collapsed from ball one.
- **CSK XI was 55% wrong:** We predicted the wrong XI for CSK in 5 of 11 positions. Ayush Mhatre, Matthew Short, Matt Henry were not in our analysis at all. This is the third consecutive game with major XI surprises.
- **Underconfidence on RR:** 55% was too low. Scenario A (40% weight, 68% RR win probability within that scenario) was the one that played out, and even within that scenario we only had RR at 68%. The actual margin (8 wickets, 47 balls remaining) suggests 70%+ was more appropriate.
- **Samson as top CSK scorer was wrong:** Predicted 30-50 runs, got 6(7). Facing his old team backfired — emotional narrative didn't translate to performance.
- **Bishnoi as top RR wicket-taker was wrong:** Three bowlers took 2 wickets each (Archer, Burger, Jadeja). The game was over too quickly for any one bowler to dominate.
- **Suryavanshi's explosion not predicted:** We had Jaiswal as RR's top scorer. Suryavanshi's 52*(17) with a 15-ball fifty was the match-defining innings and we didn't flag it.

### Single biggest error:

**First innings score range (175–190 vs actual 127).** This was a base rate anchoring error — we anchored too heavily to the venue average (~180) without adequately weighting the probability of a batting collapse. CSK fielded 4+ players we didn't predict, and their reshuffled lineup had never batted together. The "debutants struggle" scenario should have carried a lower score floor (110–140 range was possible). Our range was too narrow and too high.

### Secondary error:

**XI prediction accuracy.** For the third consecutive game, we missed multiple players in the actual XI. Step 0 (live squad news check) has been added to instructions but was not available for this game. This cascades: wrong XI → wrong matchup analysis → wrong sub-predictions.
