### Rationale for Classification

This is the second IPL 2026 match at Chepauk. Game 007 (CSK vs PBKS on April 3) was played on a fresh pitch and produced a high-scoring match (CSK 209/5, PBKS 210/5). The pitch has now been used once, meaning it will exhibit dry turning characteristics on second use rather than the early-stage seaming behaviour of a fresh pitch.

According to the pitch-types taxonomy, used Chepauk pitches typically transition from seaming (fresh) to dry turning (second use) to slow and low (third+ use). With one match played, expect the red soil base to have dried further, rough patches to have developed, and spin to be the dominant feature. Early pace movement will be minimal compared to Game 007.

### Behaviour by Phase

| Phase | Expected Behaviour |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Powerplay (1-6)** | Reduced seam movement compared to fresh pitch. Ball may skid through more than it nips. Surface is already worn, so bounce is less pronounced. New ball carries to the boundary but side-ways deviation is minimal. Pace bowlers secondary threat; spinners may be introduced early by teams seeking containment. |
| **Middle overs (7-15)** | Pitch comes alive for spin. Rough patches outside off stump (off-spinners) and outside leg (leg-spinners) are developed. Turn is exaggerated. Spinners become the primary wicket-taking weapon. Pace bowlers increasingly ineffective unless using variations (cutters, slower balls). Batting becomes progressively harder. |
| **Death overs (16-20)** | Maximum turn and variable bounce. Some balls grip sharply and turn 45°+; others skid through low. Scoring is extremely difficult. Death-over execution depends on spin variations and slower balls from pace bowlers. Run rate drops significantly compared to powerplay. Teams with strong leg-spinners or off-spinners have structural advantage. |

## 4. Toss Analysis

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Historical bat-first win % at Chepauk | ~52–55% (one of few IPL venues where batting first is advantaged) |
| Historical chase win % at Chepauk | ~45–48% |
| Toss winner typically chooses | Varies by pitch state; on used pitch, bat first is preferred (pitch deteriorates further, benefits bat-first team) |
| **Toss advantage level** | **Moderate — bat-first advantage persists but mitigated by dew** |

**Toss analysis notes:**
- Chepauk is unusual: bat-first is normally advantaged (~52–55% win rate) due to deteriorating pitch in second innings.
- Second-use pitch (like tonight) amplifies bat-first advantage because the surface is already deteriorating. A team batting first can post 170–180, and the pitch becomes even harder for chasing team.
- Dew moderates this advantage. Heavy dew in the second innings helps chasing team. With TNCA's "Dew Cure" treatment, dew severity may be lower than historical estimates.
- **Expected toss choice:** Toss winner will likely choose to bat first on this used pitch, banking on:
  1. Deteriorating surface favoring first-innings batters
  2. Dew advantage to offset some of the batting-team advantage
  3. CSK home-ground experience on Chepauk's worn pitches
- If CSK wins toss, strong likelihood they bat first. If DC wins toss, uncertain (depends on their assessment of their spin vs CSK's pace).
