## Learning Priorities (Next Game)

### Priority 1: Small-Sample Recency Weighting

**Lesson:** H2H data from <5 matches (e.g., "last 2 seasons" = 4 matches) should use lower weights. Use rolling average with decay: oldest data 25%, recent data 50%, current season data 25%.

**Application to Game 009:** If two teams have met 2-3 times in 2026 IPL, weight it at 30% max. Don't treat 4-game H2H as highly predictive.

### Priority 2: Collapse-Recovery Dynamics in Chases

**Lesson:** When a team collapses early in a chase (26/4 scenario), it does NOT increase subsequent-phase difficulty uniformly. Instead:
- PP collapse (26/4 after 6 overs, RRR 10+ initially) often leads to EM recovery (batters play cautiously, get set, then accelerate).
- Model this as: "If PP Bearish (26/4), then EM likely Neutral-to-Bullish recovery" rather than "EM stays Bearish."

**Application to Game 009:** Include explicit "collapse-recovery" scenarios in Scenario Analysis output. Example: "If DC PP Bearish (25/4), then EM recovery probability 70% (Neutral) + 20% (Bullish), only 10% (continued Bearish)."

### Priority 3: "Unknown Ceiling" Player Ceiling-Setting

**Lesson:** When a player is flagged as "Unknown ceiling," research their T20 career trajectory and recent form before assigning range. Rizvi's 70* in Game 005 (DC's previous match) should have signaled "ceiling is 60-70, not 40-50."

**Application to Game 009:** Before locking prediction, cross-reference any "Unknown ceiling" player against their recent (last 3-month) T20 performances. If available, use recent form to calibrate ceiling.
