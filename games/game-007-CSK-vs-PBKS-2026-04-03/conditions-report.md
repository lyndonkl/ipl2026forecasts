### Behaviour by Phase

| Phase | Expected Behaviour |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Powerplay (1-6)** | Fresh grass coverage will aid pace bowlers with some lateral seam movement. Ball may nip off a good length. Bounce is true and carry is good. As the pitch is fresh, early pace dominance is expected. Spinners secondary role in this phase. |
| **Middle overs (7-15)** | As the surface dries and ball loses shine, pitch transitions toward dry turning characteristics. Seam movement diminishes. Spinners begin to extract turn and grip. Rough patches develop outside off stump (for off-spinners) and outside leg (for leg-spinners). Pace bowlers become less penetrative; containment through accuracy becomes key. |
| **Death overs (16-20)** | Pitch flattens compared to middle overs but variable bounce emerges. Dry conditions mean some balls grip sharply and turn, others skid through low. Death-over scoring becomes difficult unless batters are on top of the bowlers. Spinners remain effective; slower balls and cutters from medium-pacers are dangerous. |
