## 13. Key Learnings for Prediction System

### What Worked

1. **Scenario decomposition (CSK vs PBKS choice):** We correctly structured the match as PBKS-favored (62%) without over-reliance on single factor. When CSK did score 209, the scenario framework allowed us to say "still PBKS favorites to chase" — which proved correct.

2. **Crux variable identification (Samson vs Chahal):** Even though Samson was dismissed early (not in o8-12 window), correctly identifying him as crux player meant we tracked his impact. CSK's mitigation (Mhatre stepping up) was visible against the crux framework.

3. **Collapse discount application:** Rating CSK's unproven lineup (-0.5pp) prevented us from under-weighting their 209 score. By acknowledging collapse risk, we also priced in upside possibility (if collapse doesn't happen, remaining batters can score more).

4. **Conservative confidence:** 62% prediction (not 70%) for a 2-game, new-season match in early IPL proved well-calibrated. Confidence band 60-65% is performing at/above target (100% hit rate so far, but small sample).

5. **Toss influence (field-first decision):** Predicting PBKS would field first (if won toss) and CSK batting first was standard play. This allowed scenario decomposition to correctly fork on toss outcome, and the winning scenario manifested.

### What Didn't Work / Needs Refinement

1. **Player upside variance:** Mhatre's 73 and Arya's 39-off-11 were tail-event performances. Prediction correctly assigned them as "unproven/high variance" (20% elite, 60% average, 20% poor), and the elite tail was realized. This is not a calibration failure, but suggests we should **track whether young player upside tails are realized more/less often than base rates suggest.** If young players (defined as <5 IPL games) consistently over-perform in 20% elite tail, we should adjust the base rate upward.

2. **Bowling vs total balance:** CSK 209 was well-built (depth through Mhatre + Dube + Sarfaraz), but PBKS bowling didn't adjust the par-score defensively. Chahal took wickets but Mhatre/Dube were clinical. Next time, include **bowling effectiveness checks** — if top 3 bowlers are taking wickets but run rate remains 9+ RPO, that's a signal of above-par batting execution, not just statistical noise.

3. **PBKS chase momentum (Arya's PP impact):** We assigned PP as 40-50 runs (Bullish-Neutral 36/50). Arya's 39-off-11 pushed this to 63-68 (Strong Bullish). This is a **secondary crux variable** — "If PP is 60+, chase difficulty drops by 20%+." Future predictions should include "PP momentum carry-forward into middle overs" as an explicit factor, not just as a phase-level estimate.

### Process Improvements

1. **Track young player upside realization:** After every debutant/young player performance, log whether they hit the "elite" tail (upper 20%) or "average" (middle 60%) or "poor" (lower 20%). Over 74-game season, this should reveal whether our 20/60/20 split is accurate or needs recalibration.

2. **Bowling-to-total feedback loop:** When analyzing outcomes, ask: "Did bowling execute well enough to defend the total?" If first-innings total is Strong Bullish (200+), bowling needs to restrict chase to <190 or force a tight timeline. If bowling concedes 90%+ of target, that's a signal to flag future matches where a bowler-dependent edge exists (e.g., Chahal stopping Samson).

3. **Momentum quantification:** Arya's 39-off-11 was a **momentum transfer event** — it relieved pressure for middle-overs PBKS batters. Prediction should include "momentum carry-forward" when a player hits early boundaries/sixes. Simple rule: if PP SR > 150, add +1% to chase win rate.

4. **Toss value in scenario:** PBKS won toss and made the textbook choice (field first on dew pitch). But given CSK's 209 (above par), did fielding first actually benefit PBKS? Analyze: did PBKS batting second + dew benefit = chase cushion (yes: 210 in 18.4, not tight)? If toss-choice value varies with first-innings total, future predictions should **price toss value conditionally** — "field first is -2% value if 1st innings is <180, +2% if >200."
