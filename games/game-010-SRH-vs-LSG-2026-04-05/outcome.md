### Brier Score Calculation

**Formula:** (Probability assigned to actual winner - 1)²

**Calculation:** LSG won. We assigned LSG 49%. Brier = (0.49 - 1)² = (-0.51)² = **0.2601**

**Benchmark:**
- 0.1369 = good (Game 001, 002)
- 0.15 = target range
- 0.25 = random coin-flip
- 0.2601 = our Game 010 result

**Interpretation:** Brier of 0.2601 is slightly above the "random coin-flip" threshold of 0.25. The prediction was near-50-50, LSG won, and we assigned only 49% to LSG. This is a modest prediction error, not a catastrophic miss.

## 7. Prediction Narrative Review

### What Went Right

1. **Market Divergence Identified:** We correctly identified that bookmakers favored SRH (58%) while our model showed 51/49. The 7 pp gap was a genuine edge signal for LSG.

2. **Toss Advantage Neutralized:** We correctly assessed that afternoon start (no dew) made toss impact negligible, preventing overweighting of SRH's home advantage.

3. **Confidence Level Appropriate:** Medium confidence was correct — LSG XI uncertainty (Hasaranga fitness), small H2H sample, and symmetrical strengths all warranted caution.

4. **Phase Decomposition Structure:** Our phase-based analysis (PP, Middle, Death) correctly identified that LSG's depth in middle overs (Pooran/Marsh) could offset SRH's powerplay edge.

### What Went Wrong

1. **Powerplay Edge Underestimated:** We modeled SRH powerplay as "Bullish 35%," implying 65% of outcomes are Neutral-to-Bearish. But the actual Bearish outcome (3 wkts in 3.3 overs) was more severe than our distribution expected.

2. **Shami's Post-Surgery Quality:** We treated Shami as a "known quantity" at expected 7.5–8.0 economy. His 2/9 MOTM was exceptional and not fully captured in our credence intervals.

3. **Form Surge Confidence:** Klaasen + Nitish's combined 118-run partnership in middle overs exceeded our 75–85 range. We were too conservative on recent form (Game 006 surge), should have widened expected range to 80–120.

4. **Pant "Form Dip" Weight:** We flagged Pant's SR 133 in 2025 as a weakness. His 68* (SR 136) in the chase showed the concern was overblown. Recent data (1 game) vs historical trend weighting needs recalibration.

### Biggest Single Error

**SRH's powerplay collapse (26 runs, 3 wkts) was the match-determining factor.** This -24 to -34 run swing was not fully accommodated in our "Bearish 25%" scenario. The distribution should have included a "Extreme Bearish" tail (~10%) covering collapses of this magnitude.

**Why did we miss this?** We weighted recent form (Game 006: SRH 226/8) heavily as a "form surge" signal, but applied it symmetrically to both Opening and Middle phases. The powerplay collapse in Game 010 suggests that Shami's elite execution against aggressive openers can disrupt form surges quickly. We should have modeled **option value** — Shami's upside as a bowler was as high as SRH's opening upside as batters.

## 9. Kushal's Learning Log Entry

### What to Log

1. **Form Misjudge on Pant:** Pant's SR 133 in 2025 was real but not predictive of 2026 performance. In future predictions, use at least 3 recent games (not just 1 or 2) before flagging "form dip."

2. **Shami's Post-Surgery Upside:** When a player returns from injury after significant downtime, credence intervals should be **wider, not narrower.** We treated Shami as "known quantity," but post-surgery performance can be both elite (as here) and unpredictable. Next time: +2-3 pp wider error bars.

3. **Powerplay Tail Risk:** Our "Bearish 25%" for SRH powerplay should have included a "Extreme Bearish" sub-scenario (~10%) for collapses exceeding -20 runs. The 26 run total was an outcome we modeled as possible, but our distribution didn't weight it high enough.

4. **Middle-Order Form Carries Forward:** Klaasen + Nitish's combined 118 runs (SR 160+) confirmed that Game 006's "form surge" is real and should be weighted more heavily in confidence intervals. In future, use rolling 3-game average for recent form rather than point estimates.

5. **Trade thesis vindication:** The +5¢ edge on LSG was correct, and the win at $0.42 (vs our 49% model) validated the Kalshi decision. However, the mechanism (early SRH collapse + Pant dominance) differed from expected (close game throughout). This is fine — edge doesn't require path accuracy, only outcome accuracy.
