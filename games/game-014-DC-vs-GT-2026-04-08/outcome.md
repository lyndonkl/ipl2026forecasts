## Section 8 — Prediction Narrative Review

### What the Model Got Right

1. **Direction on toss decision (Branch B):** DC won toss, chose to bowl first (Branch B scenario). The locked probability (50.0% DC for Branch B) proved nearly exact — DC came within 1 run.

2. **Chase viability:** The prediction rated the chase at 50.0% (approximately even odds). DC's actual pursuit (209 vs 211) validates this assessment. The closing margin (1 run) proves the probability was well-calibrated.

3. **GT death bowling edge:** Despite missing Rabada, GT's death performance (26 runs in 3 overs, forcing a 1-run victory) aligned with the predicted LR 1.25 (GT edges). The margin of victory itself validates the edge rating.

4. **Flat pitch + high-scoring:** The model predicted 175-190 par range; both teams exceeded this (210 and 209). The flat Arun Jaitley pitch + aggressive intent confirmed the prediction's par-score calibration.

### What the Model Got Wrong

1. **Rashid Khan's true impact:** The v3 downweight (LR 0.88 → 0.95) underestimated Rashid's 3-wicket game-deciding performance. This was the single largest model error by impact.

2. **Kuldeep's applicability:** The upweighting assumed Phillips would face Kuldeep in specific overs; Phillips' actual batting line didn't align with the weighted overs. Over-confident in a matchup that never materialized.

3. **Late-order acceleration pattern:** Washington Sundar (55 off 32) + Buttler (52 off 27) consecutive acceleration was not modeled as a distinct two-batter threat. Agents treat these as individual players, not threat pairings.

4. **Player Research gaps:** Rizvi's vulnerability to left-arm pace (Prasidh) was not surfaced pre-match. Miller's retirement-hurt was noted but pessimistically framed.

### Biggest Regret

**The Rashid Khan downweight (override #2) cost us ~2-3pp of calibration.** The original agent LR (0.88) was closer to truth than our "thin sample" discount (0.95). ## Section 12 — Lessons Integration & Recurring Patterns

### New Patterns Identified in Game 014

#### Pattern 1: "Late-Order Pairing Threat Underestimated"

| Game | Manifestation | Example |
|------|---|---|
| Game 004 (PBKS vs GT) | PBKS tail-order pairing (Malik + Tewatia) accelerated late | — |
| **Game 014** | **GT late-order (Sundar + Buttler + Gill retained) accelerated 110 runs in LM+Death** | Sundar 55 + Buttler 52 = 107 runs |

**Systemic Pattern:** The Scenario Analysis Agent models each batter independently and assigns LRs by phase. But when two world-class power-hitters bat in consecutive phases (EM + LM or MO + Death), the agent doesn't capture the "momentum carry" or "partnership dominance." The pairing acts like a multiplier, not a sum of individuals.

**Suggested Process Fix:** Player Research should surface "dangerous pairing" flags (e.g., "Sundar + Buttler bat 7-17; both score 150+ SR in T20; paired threat >50 runs expected"). Scenario agent should then apply a "pairing multiplier" (×1.1 or ×1.2) to the late-order LRs when two such batters are present.

---

#### Pattern 2: "Downweight-Based-on-Sample-Size Backfires When Player Has Track Record"

| Game | Example | Outcome |
|------|---------|---------|
| Game 006 (KKR vs SRH) | Sunrisers spinners downweighted due to missing data | Spinners dominated; bad downweight |
| **Game 014** | Rashid Khan downweighted (thin 2026 sample) vs original LR 0.88 | Rashid took 3 wickets; downweight was wrong |

**Systemic Pattern:** When a player has strong career/historical IPL form but thin early-season 2026 sample, the uncertainty discount (pull toward 0.5 / neutral) is over-applied. The agent should anchor to career track record, not short-term sample.

**Suggested Process Fix:** For spinners with <3 games in IPL 2026, use a weighted-average: 70% historical IPL form + 30% early-season performance. Don't pure downweight. Rashid's career dominance (avg 4-5 wkts/game in T20 franchises) should have overridden the thin-sample concern.

---

#### Pattern 3: "XI Information Gap on Specific Bowlers Cascades to Death LR"

| Game | Gap | Impact |
|------|-----|--------|
| Game 004 (PBKS vs GT) | Holder's non-selection (predicted to play) | Death bowling adjustment was wrong |
| **Game 014** | Rabada's non-selection (not flagged, but assumed) | Death edge prediction was fragile |

**Systemic Pattern:** When a critical bowler (Rabada, Holder) is pre-selected in the model but doesn't play, the death-overs LR cascades incorrectly through the entire phase analysis. The model doesn't flag "death bowling contingent on Rabada's selection."

**Suggested Process Fix:** Team Research Agent should surface "death bowling contingency: if [bowler] plays, LR = X; if doesn't play, LR = Y." This flags the uncertainty to downstream agents.
