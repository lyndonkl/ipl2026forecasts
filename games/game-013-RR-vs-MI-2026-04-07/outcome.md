## Lessons & Calibration Flags

### 1. Process Lesson: Real Market Quotes Are Non-Negotiable

**What went wrong in prediction.md:**
- Step 1 base rate was synthesized qualitatively ("RR 55%" from narrative signals)
- Actual Kalshi at decision time was RR 43¢ / MI 57¢
- This 12pp divergence was ONLY discovered when Kushal looked up Kalshi for trade decision
- Prediction was already locked at 57.8%, so the divergence was never incorporated into the confidence band

**Going forward:**
- **Rule:** ALWAYS get a real Kalshi quote (or pure bookmaker odds, e.g., Betfair) before computing base rate.
- **Process:** In Step 1 (Pitch Report), after all team/player info is gathered, immediately web-search for Kalshi market or bookmaker odds.
- **If no quote available:** Flag the base rate as provisional and do not lock final confidence until market data is obtained.

**Impact:** This was a process flaw, but the outcome (RR won) means it didn't harm P&L. If the prediction had been wrong, this would have been identified as a causal gap. **Frequency tracking:** Next 5 predictions should all include real market quotes in Step 1.

---

### 2. Scenario Lesson: 11-Over Matches Compress Phase Dynamics

**What happened:**
- Prediction structured Scenario A (RR bats first) into 4 phases: PP (1-6), EM (7-12), LM (13-17), Death (18-20)
- Actual match was 11 overs, compressing phases to: PP (1-3.33), EM (4-7.33), LM (8-9.33), Death (10-11)
- RR's aggressive PP (59 runs in 3.2 overs, SR 175+) front-loaded scoring, making EM/LM/Death less decisive
- Prediction's LRs for EM (Bishnoi 1.20) and Death (RR 0.85 = vulnerable to Bumrah) never materialized as modeled

**Going forward:**
- **Rule:** For rain-affected 11-overs formats, adjust phase LRs downward for slow/spin bowlers (they get fewer high-impact overs) and upward for aggressive batters (compressed format favors early hitting).
- **Suggested adjustment:** -10% to -15% for medium-overs LRs in 11-over matches; +5% to +10% for aggressive batters in compressed formats.
- **Practice:** In next rain-affected prediction, explicitly compute two scenario branches: (A) Full 20-over match, (B) Reduced 11-over match, and update LRs accordingly.

### 3. Archer Impact: Underestimated in MI Chase Scenario

**What went wrong:**
- Prediction flagged Archer as a threat to MI openers in Branch B ("Archer 150+ kph threatens Rohit/Rickelton, but explosive potential")
- LR for MI PP in Branch B was set to 1.15 (MI slight edge), implying both Rickelton and Rohit would be volatile but competitive
- Actual: Rickelton 0 (duck), Rohit 2* (3 balls), SKY 2* (3 balls) = 4 runs in first 3 overs
- Archer's impact was MASSIVE (3-4 early wickets), not "slight edge" territory

**Root cause:** LR 1.15 conflated volatility (Rohit could score 25 or 10 randomly) with matchup performance (Archer is elite fast bowler vs opening batters in PowerPlay). These are different factors.

**Going forward:**
- **Rule:** When assigning LRs for pace bowler vs batter matchups, separate:
  1. Batter form volatility (do they have a wide distribution of scores, yes/no?)
  2. Specific bowler-batter matchup history (has this bowler dominated this batter, yes/no?)
  3. Format/conditions effects (new-ball seam, dew, pitch pace)
- **For Archer vs RR openers in Branch B:** Should have been LR 0.85–0.90 (RR advantage due to Archer elite), not 1.15 (MI slight advantage).
- **Lesson:** Archer 3-4 early wickets is forecastable if you model the matchup correctly. This was a reasoning miss, not random noise.
