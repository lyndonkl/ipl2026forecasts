| **Impact on 2nd innings bowling** | **Heavy — substantial impact on bowling effectiveness, particularly spin** |

### Dew Impact Analysis

Dew becomes a major factor from overs 12–13 onwards in the second innings. With temperatures expected to cool from 34°C to 24–26°C and humidity at 69%, the ball will become progressively slippery. This affects:

- **Spinners:** Grip diminishes significantly by mid-second innings. Leg-spinners and off-spinners lose control on length and line. Death-over bowling by spinners becomes high-risk.
- **Pacers:** Reverse swing becomes difficult. Conventional seam bowling and cutters more reliable than attempts at reverse swing.
- **Fielders:** Catching becomes hazardous with a wet ball. Misfields and dropped catches likely.
- **Batters (second innings):** Ball skids onto the bat more readily, aiding strokeplay and reducing bowling effectiveness.
