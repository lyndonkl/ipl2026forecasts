**This is a 15:30 IST start, NOT a typical 19:30 IST evening start.** Standard dew onset guidance (8:00–8:30 PM at Chinnaswamy) applies to NIGHT matches with later start times and extended afternoon-to-evening playing windows.

| **Second innings death (Overs 16–20)** | 20:30–21:15 | Heavy dew likely | Medium confidence |

### Key Findings

1. **First innings: Dew is NOT a factor.** Both teams play under dry conditions throughout.
2. **Early second innings: Dew is negligible.** If match reaches overs 7–15 of 2nd innings by 20:30 IST, dew may aid second-innings batting, but will NOT be dominant through the middle overs.
3. **Late second innings: Dew becomes relevant only.** Final 5 overs (overs 16–20 of 2nd innings) face possible dew, but by then most wickets are typically resolved.
4. **Afternoon-start dew advantage is weaker than night-match dew advantage.** The usual "dew makes chasing much easier" narrative is significantly diminished for 15:30 starts.

### Impact on Bowling

If dew does manifest (late in 2nd innings, overs 18–20+ only):
- **Spinners lose grip:** Off-spinners and leg-spinners harder to execute; arm-ball and googly become unreliable.
- **Fast bowlers gain marginal advantage:** Slippery ball makes slower balls and yorkers harder to control but also harder for batters to pick.
- **Death bowling variation is key:** Dew exacerbates the need for precise slower-ball execution.

**Conclusion:** Dew is a **minor, late-game factor** for this afternoon-start match. Normal conditions dominate the first 30+ overs of play. Toss advantage (if any) is NOT significantly amplified by dew as it would be in a night match.
