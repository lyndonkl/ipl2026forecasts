## MI — Batting Unit

### Opening Pair: Rohit Sharma + Quinton de Kock

**Rohit Sharma (RHB)**
- 967 career runs vs KKR — dominates this matchup historically.
- Powerplay aggressor. Attacks from ball one at Wankhede.
- Weakness: Can be beaten by high pace and bounce early. But Wankhede's
  true surface suits his pull shot and front-foot driving.

**Quinton de Kock (LHB, wk)**
- Left-handed — creates variety in the opening pair.
- Aggressive powerplay batter. Career IPL SR ~135.
- Against Varun Chakravarthy: Left-hander vs off-spin mystery — could be
  a key middle-overs matchup if de Kock bats deep.

**Opening pair assessment:** Elite. Both are proven IPL match-winners. At
Wankhede against a weakened KKR pace attack, expect an aggressive powerplay.
Expected powerplay: 50–62 if both survive.

### Top Order (3–4): SKY + Tilak Varma

**Suryakumar Yadav (RHB)**
- 617 career runs vs KKR. IPL 2025: 583 runs at SR 170.46.
- 360-degree batter — can score anywhere on the ground.
- Wankhede is his home ground. Knows every blade of grass.
- Weakness: Can play rash shots when trying to dominate spin.
  Varun Chakravarthy could be his biggest threat tonight.

**Tilak Varma (LHB)**
- Young left-hander. Excellent middle-overs batter. Can anchor or
  accelerate depending on the situation.
- Provides left-hand/right-hand combination with SKY.

### Middle Order / Finishers (5–7): Hardik, Rutherford, Shardul

**Hardik Pandya (c, RHB/RM)**
- Designated finisher + 5th bowler. If fit to bowl 4 overs, MI's balance
  is ideal. If not bowling, MI are one bowler short.
- Death-over power hitter. Can clear any boundary.

**Sherfane Rutherford (LHB)**
- West Indian power hitter. Left-handed variety at 6.
- Replacing Will Jacks — slightly less versatile but more power.

### Batting Depth Assessment
MI bat deep to No.8 (Deepak Chahar can bat in emergencies). Top 6 are
all capable of 30+ strike-rate innings. At Wankhede, this lineup should
chase 175+ with confidence.

## KKR — Batting Unit

### Opening Pair: Finn Allen + Ajinkya Rahane (c)

**Finn Allen (RHB)**
- NZ explosive opener. IPL SR ~155. Attacks from ball one.
- Against Bumrah in the powerplay: HIGH RISK. Bumrah's pace and bounce
  vs Allen's aggressive approach = fireworks or early dismissal.
- Wankhede's true bounce suits his pull and cut shots.

**Ajinkya Rahane (c, RHB)**
- IPL 2025: 390 runs at SR 147.72 — better than his career average.
- Traditional concern: Career T20 SR ~120–125 may be too slow for
  modern IPL. But recent seasons suggest he's adapted.
- Role: Anchor while Allen attacks. Must not consume too many dot balls.

**Opening pair assessment:** Mismatched styles. Allen provides aggression,
Rahane provides stability. Risk: if Allen falls early, Rahane's anchor
approach under Bumrah/Boult pressure could leave KKR 30/1 off 4 overs.
Expected powerplay: 42–55 (lower than MI due to Bumrah/Boult threat).

### Middle Order (3–5): Green, Raghuvanshi, Rinku

**Cameron Green (LHB/RMF)**
- Record IPL purchase at 25.20 cr. Batting + bowling AR.
- Left-handed — creates angle variety against MI's right-arm-heavy pace.
- Test-match technique. Can anchor but also accelerate (SR ~140 in T20Is).
- Bowling: 130–135 kph seam. Gives KKR a 4th pace option. This is
  critical given the pace crisis.
- IPL debut pressure at the most expensive overseas contract ever.

**Angkrish Raghuvanshi**
- Young Indian batter. Limited IPL data. Unknown quantity.

**Rinku Singh (LHB)**
- KKR's designated finisher. IPL 2023–24 hero (remember 5 sixes off
  Yash Dayal). Left-handed, excellent in the death.
- Against Bumrah at the death: One of the toughest matchups in cricket.
  Bumrah vs finishers is the ultimate test.

### Batting Depth Assessment
KKR bat to No.7 (Narine). After Rinku, the batting drops off
significantly — Ramandeep and Narine can hit but are unreliable.
Varun at 8 is a genuine tailender with the bat. If KKR lose 4 wickets
by over 14, their total is in trouble.

## MI — Bowling Unit

### Powerplay Bowlers (Overs 1–6)

**Jasprit Bumrah (right-arm fast, 145+ kph)**
- 25 career wickets vs KKR. ELITE.
- Powerplay: Economy ~7.0. Bounce and accuracy are devastating.
- Against Allen: If Bumrah bowls the first over, expect a high-stakes
  battle. Allen will attack; Bumrah will test his technique.
- Against Rahane: Bumrah will tie Rahane down with dot balls. Rahane's
  lower SR makes him vulnerable to pressure from Bumrah's accuracy.

**Trent Boult (left-arm fast, 135–140 kph)**
- Powerplay swing specialist. Left-arm angle across right-handers.
- New ball at Wankhede with 74% humidity = potential swing.
- Against Allen (RHB): Boult's inswing to a right-hander could trouble
  Allen early.
- IPL 2025: 19 wickets, economy 8.38.

**Deepak Chahar (right-arm medium-fast, swing)**
- May bowl 1–2 overs in the powerplay for variation.
- Swing bowler — humidity helps.

### Middle Overs Bowlers (Overs 7–15)

**MI's weakness.** Without Santner or a quality spinner, MI rely on:
- Shardul Thakur (seam)
- Hardik Pandya (medium pace)
- Whoever fills the spinner slot (Markande?)
- Part-time options

Against Narine + Varun bowling for KKR in the middle overs, MI's own
middle-over bowling may be the weak link. If KKR's batters survive the
powerplay, they could score freely in overs 7–15.

### Death Over Specialists (Overs 16–20)

**Jasprit Bumrah — ELITE death bowler**
- Career death-over economy: sub-8.0. Best in IPL history.
- Yorkers, bouncers, slower balls — complete death-over package.
- Against Rinku Singh: The ultimate IPL matchup.
- Will bowl overs 17 and 19 (penultimate + final is the typical
  Bumrah deployment).

**Trent Boult**
- Good death bowler. Left-arm yorkers into right-handers.
- Economy rises at the death but still effective.

**Overall MI bowling assessment:** Pace-dominant and lethal. Bumrah alone
swings this department massively in MI's favour. The weakness is spin
in the middle overs — KKR's batters may target this phase.

## KKR — Bowling Unit

### Powerplay Bowlers (Overs 1–6)

**Blessing Muzarabani (right-arm fast, 140+ kph)**
- Strong T20 World Cup. Hits the deck, extracts bounce.
- IPL debut tonight. No IPL-specific data. But a genuine international
  fast bowler — not a makeshift option.
- Against Rohit: Muzarabani's bounce could test Rohit early. But Rohit
  is one of the best pullers in cricket — risky battle for the bowler.

**Vaibhav Arora (right-arm fast-medium)**
- IPL 2025: 17 wickets — KKR's joint-highest. A reliable domestic option.
- Not express pace but accurate. Good new-ball bowler.

**Cameron Green (right-arm medium-fast, 130–135 kph)**
- May bowl 1 over in the powerplay. Crucial given KKR need overs from
  somewhere. His 3–4 overs of seam could be the difference between a
  functional attack and a disintegrated one.

### Middle Overs Bowlers (Overs 7–15)

**Varun Chakravarthy (mystery off-spin)**
- KKR's BEST weapon tonight. IPL 2025: 17 wickets.
- Mystery variations: carrom ball, googly, off-break.
- Against MI's right-handers (Rohit, SKY, Hardik): If the Wankhede
  pitch offers anything, Varun could be decisive.
- Flat pitch modifier: Minimal turn expected. Varun must rely on
  variations and flight rather than purchase off the surface.

**Sunil Narine (off-spin/mystery)**
- Will bowl 4 overs — economic and containing.
- Against Rohit: Narine has 26 career wickets vs MI. The most successful
  KKR bowler against Mumbai.
- Role: Contain in the middle overs while Varun takes wickets.

### Death Over Specialists (Overs 16–20)

**KKR's biggest vulnerability.** Without Pathirana and Mustafizur, who
bowls the death?
- Muzarabani (untested in IPL death overs but has the pace)
- Kartik Tyagi (inconsistent, can be expensive)
- Green (medium-fast — risky at the death against Hardik/Rutherford)

**Assessment:** KKR's death bowling is a serious liability. If MI have
5+ wickets in hand at over 16, expect MI to score 60+ in the death
regardless of what KKR bowl. This is the matchup phase that most
heavily favours MI.

## Key Individual Matchups (5 identified)

### 1. Bumrah vs Finn Allen (Powerplay)

**Head-to-head:** Limited direct data (below 15 balls).
**Bowling-style proxy:** Allen vs right-arm fast 145+ — SR ~130. Allen is
aggressive but genuine pace and bounce can beat him.
**Assessment:** Moderate advantage Bumrah. Allen will attack but Bumrah's
accuracy at 145+ creates genuine dismissal chances.
**Prediction impact:** +2% for MI.

### 2. Narine vs MI's batting (Middle Overs)

**Head-to-head:** Narine has 26 career wickets vs MI — his best record
against any team.
**Assessment:** Narine is historically effective against MI. But MI's
current lineup (SKY, Tilak) are better players of spin than MI's
historical lineups. Flat Wankhede pitch reduces Narine's advantage.
**Prediction impact:** +1% for KKR.

### 3. Varun Chakravarthy vs Rohit/SKY (Middle Overs)

**Bowling-style proxy:** Rohit vs mystery spin — historically scores freely
(SR ~140) but can be deceived by the carrom ball. SKY attacks spin
aggressively — high-risk/high-reward.
**Assessment:** Even matchup. Varun could take a key wicket or get
smashed for 40 off 4 overs. On a flat pitch, slight batter advantage.
**Prediction impact:** Neutral.

### 4. Bumrah vs Rinku Singh (Death Overs)

**Assessment:** The marquee death-overs matchup. Bumrah's yorker accuracy
vs Rinku's ability to hit under pressure. Bumrah holds the advantage in
most conditions. At Wankhede, short boundaries give Rinku a chance on
mishits.
**Prediction impact:** +2% for MI. Bumrah's death bowling is the single
biggest individual advantage in this match.

### 5. Green's all-round impact

**Assessment:** Green's contribution with both bat AND ball determines
KKR's competitiveness. If Green scores 40+ AND bowls 3 economical overs,
KKR can compete. If he fails with both, KKR's structural weakness is
fully exposed.
**Prediction impact:** If Green delivers: +3% KKR. If Green fails: -3% KKR.
Modelled as neutral (uncertainty cancels out).
