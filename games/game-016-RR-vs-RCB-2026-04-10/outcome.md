**Trade:** 2 contracts NO RCB @ 47¢ = $0.94 cost + ~$0.04 fee = ~$0.98 total cost
**Thesis:** RR 52% edge means RCB 48%. Kalshi implied RCB 53%, so +5pp edge. Buy NO RCB at 47¢.

### Trade Thesis Evaluation

**Was the thesis sound?** Yes. RR 52% / RCB 48% vs market implied RCB 53%, so +5pp edge. Expected value:
- **If RR wins (52% chance):** RCB NO pays $1.00. Gain per contract: $1.00 - $0.47 = +$0.53
- **If RCB wins (48% chance):** RCB NO pays $0. Loss per contract: -$0.47
- **EV = 0.52 × (+$0.53) + 0.48 × (-$0.47) = +$0.2756 - $0.2256 = +$0.05 per contract**

EV was +$0.05 per contract, or ~+$0.10 for 2 contracts before fees (~$0.04). Net edge ~+$0.06. **Thesis was sound and the edge was real — small but positive after fees.**

**Did Kalshi market price RCB correctly?** Kalshi NO RCB at 47¢ implies RCB 53% to win (YES RCB = 53¢). Our model had RCB at 48%. The 5pp gap was the edge. Given RR's 3-0 form streak and bowling depth advantage, the market was slightly overpricing RCB.

**Would we make the same trade again?** Yes. Thesis was correct (RR won), edge was positive, sizing was appropriate. The trade executed as planned — bought the right side at the right price.
