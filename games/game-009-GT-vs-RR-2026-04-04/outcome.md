## Key Lessons Learned

### Prediction System Gaps

1. **XI Confirmation Timing:** Trade lock-in should occur POST-toss with confirmed XI. Pre-toss trades on thin information carry hidden XI risk. (Gill's absence evaporated the 56% thesis.)

2. **Player Ceiling Estimation:** Early-season predictions on new IPL entrants (Bishnoi, Suryavanshi) or returning players with injury uncertainty (Sudharsan) should use wider ranges. Model was overly confident on conservative midpoints.

3. **Form vs. Data Gaps:** Jurel (75 off 42) was known T20 performer but IPL-specific form was underweighted. Prediction model relied on IPL historical data when recent T20 WC 2026 form was more predictive. Bias toward stable priors over recent data.

4. **Market Efficiency:** Kalshi's 50/50 pricing (vs our 56%) was closer to fair value given XI uncertainty and dew dynamics. Thin edges (6pp) require extremely high confidence to trade; Medium confidence should trigger pass or half-sizing.

- Game 003: RR model prediction (55%) outperformed market (50/50), profitable trade
- Game 005: Correct prediction despite choosing PASS on trade (edge too thin)
- Games 004, 006: Incorrect predictions but Medium confidence prevented over-sizing

**Pattern:** Prediction system is accurate 5 of 7 games (71%) but is calibrating better on when to trade (Game 007 showed discipline in PBKS high-confidence edge). Game 009 loss is a setback but consistent with Medium-confidence range.
