## 5. Brier Score Calculation

**Formula:**  
Brier = (probability assigned to losing team)²

**Calculation:**
- Locked prediction: MI 54.5% / RCB 45.5%
- Actual winner: RCB
- Probability assigned to loser (MI): 0.545
- Brier Score = (0.545)² = **0.2970**

**Interpretation:**
- Brier 0.2970 = "Moderately confident and wrong."

## 8. Learning Log Entry

**Date:** 2026-04-12  
**Game:** 020 | **Teams:** MI vs RCB | **Venue:** Wankhede | **Result:** RCB +18r | **Brier:** 0.2970

### Key Lessons

1. **Extreme form flags require scenario-specific modeling:**
   - Padikkal's +65% EM dominance was predicted for RCB **second innings** (chasing, dew-assisted).
   - RCB **first innings** (batting first, dry pitch), Padikkal's dominance was not explicitly modeled into total expectation.
   - **Fix:** When extreme form is flagged, model its application to **all scenarios** the batter will face, not just primary scenario. Padikkal +65% EM is a bat-side phenomenon, applies to EM whenever RCB bats (1st or 2nd innings).

2. **First-innings par setting underaccounts for upside tail:**
   - Par set at 180-195 (based on Wankhede historical 170-175 + modern IPL elevation).
   - Actual: 240 (39% above top of par).
   - **Root cause:** Model anchored par to historical benchmarks and Phase probabilities (Bullish 12%, Above Par 26%, Par 38% from scenario-analysis.md). Did NOT sufficiently weight 5-6 extreme form players all hitting in same innings.
   - **Fix:** When 50%+ of batting lineup has form flags ±30%+, increase par-expectation ceiling by 20-30 runs (account for correlation in extreme form). Adjust probabilities: Blowout 15-20%, Above-Par 35%, Par 30%, Below-Par 15%.

3. **Rohit's early exit (retirement hurt) was unpredictable but highlights dependency concentration:**
   - Model heavily weighted MI's PP edge on Rohit +38%.
   - Rohit's retirement hurt (overs 8-9) disrupted this edge.
   - **Lesson:** MI's PP edge was over-concentrated on single batter. Rickelton (also out 7.1), Tilak (7.5) amplified collapse. Three openers/middle-order down by overs 7-8 = chase unrecoverable.
   - **Fix:** Flag concentration risk when >50% of phase LR comes from ≤2 batters. Diversify LR sources or adjust LR magnitude downward.

4. **Market pricing (Kalshi MI 53¢) reflected edge uncertainty:**
   - Our 54.5% was just 1.5pp above Kalshi 53¢.
   - RCB's actual 45.5% (implicit in our model) was mispriced higher than market's 47¢ by only 1.5pp.
   - This razor-thin edge suggests market (crowd-sourced betting) had better granularity on Wankhede dew/chase advantage than our model's toss-conditional weighting.
   - **Lesson:** Thin edges (1-2pp) after fee elimination are appropriately PASSED. Market was "right-ish" on overall probability; our edge was in the noise band.

5. **5-game rolling Brier: 0.2605 (above 0.25 threshold) — calibration review triggered:**
   - Games 016-020 Brier scores: [0.2304, 0.1764, 0.4225, 0.1764, 0.2970]
   - Average: 0.2605 > 0.25 (trigger threshold).
   - Two large misses: Game 018 (CSK upset, Brier 0.4225) and Game 020 (RCB, Brier 0.2970).
   - **Action:** Proceed to Calibration Review after completing Game 020 debriefing.
