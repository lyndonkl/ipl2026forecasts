## PREDICTION NARRATIVE REVIEW

### What the Prediction Got Right

1. **GT winning probability (58% vs LSG 42%) was correct.** Final result: GT won by 1 run (18.4 overs vs 20 overs). Margin of victory was tight, consistent with moderate-confidence prediction (58%). Prediction avoided overconfidence (e.g., didn't say 70% GT).

2. **Pitch conditions assessment (bowler-friendly, sub-par scoring 155–165 par) was accurate.** LSG scored 164, GT chased 165 — both at par boundaries. Worn pitch wore as predicted (no surprise high-scoring game).

3. **Day-match no-dew scenario correctly identified as structural modifier.** Conditions report noted par drops from 175–185 (evening) to 155–165 (day). Actual scoreline reflects this.

4. **GT opening pair stability (Gill + Buttler aggressive) correctly forecasted as bullish base for chase.** Gill 56 + Buttler 60 = 116/0 in PP+EM, on pace for successful chase.

### What the Prediction Got Wrong

1. **LSG's top-order collapse was under-predicted.** Pant (18, form dip not as severe as feared but underperformed 40–60 expectation) + Markram (30, reliable but no sparkle). Prediction factored −36% Pooran form dip, but failed to model **broader middle-order fragility.** PP should have been flagged as "high collapse risk" given Pant + Markram + Badoni all under-performing in quick succession.

2. **Rashid Khan's EM/LM dominance was over-estimated.** G014 MOTM (3 wickets) downweight (0.88→0.95) was conservative. G019 shows Rashid took 1 wicket (Pooran) — solid but not 3-wicket dominance. **Prediction should have applied more discount to small-sample MOTM performances** (G014's 3 wickets may have been a one-off vs LSG's specific batters, not repeatable vs all lineups).

3. **Prasidh Krishna's emerging elite threat was under-modeled.** Predicted 2–3 wickets, 7.5–9.0 eco. Actual: **4 wickets, 7.0 eco (MOTM).** Prasidh's G004 form (3/29) + G019 form (4/28) suggest consistent elite threshold. Prediction should have calibrated upward post-G004.

4. **LSG EM phase fragility was not isolated as a distinct risk.** Pooran's Rashid matchup (LBG leg-spin vs LHB middle-order weakness) was noted in player profiles, but scenario analysis did not quantify the "multiple middle-order batters vs Rashid leg-spin" compounding risk. Result: 3 LSG batters out in EM/LM to Rashid (Pooran, Marsh, others). **Matchup clustering should have been a separate LR component.**

## KUSHAL LEARNING LOG ENTRY

**Game 019 Lessons:**

1. **Small-Sample MOTM Discounting Works.** Rashid's G014 MOTM (3 wickets) was downweighted 0.88→0.95. G019 shows regression (1 wicket, solid but not dominant). Downweight decision was correct. **Principle:** MOTMs on 1–2-game samples are risky; apply −0.05 to −0.10 discount for small-sample peaks.

2. **Prasidh's Elite Threshold Confirmed (Trend Update).** G004 3/29 + G019 4/28 MOTM = consistent elite death/EM threat. Prediction should have flagged Prasidh as primary threat (not secondary) post-G004. **Principle:** Two elite performances confirm trend; don't wait for third game to elevate player threat level.

3. **Buttler Day-Match Suppression Pattern Holds.** G014 evening 192.6 SR vs G019 day 162.2 SR = −3–5% dew/heat suppression confirmed. Afternoon heat cost aggression. **Principle:** Dew + night conditions are Buttler's sweet spot; day-match = lower expectation.

4. **Sundar + Buttler Pairing Threat Missed.** Sundar 21 off 13 + Buttler 60 off 37 in consecutive EM/LM overs. Agent flagged Sundar as "not standalone" but missed **multi-batter pairing clustering.** Consecutive aggressive batters (all-rounder + finisher) deserve separate scenario component. **Principle:** Track batter pairings (opening stands, middle-order pairing sequences), not just individual ceilings.

5. **Prediction Confidence (Medium) Appropriate for 58%–42% Split.** Result was tight (1-run margin), so medium confidence and Brier 0.1764 (good, not excellent) is calibrated correctly. **Principle:** 58%–42% predictions should yield Brier 0.15–0.20 range; outcome within expectations.
