## Pitch Profile

**Pitch type classification: FLAT BATTING SURFACE**

- Classic Chinnaswamy pitch — flat, bouncy, true surface with even
  carry. Good pace onto the bat. The outfield is lightning-fast.
- **Early overs (1–6):** True bounce, minimal lateral movement. May
  offer marginal assistance for pace in the first 2–3 overs with the
  new ball, but nothing significant. The ball comes onto the bat.
- **Middle overs (7–15):** Surface remains consistent. Spinners will
  get minimal turn — they must rely on flight, changes of pace, and
  accuracy rather than turn. Batters can hit through the line.
- **Death overs (16–20):** No deterioration. Death bowling execution
  is everything. The short straight boundaries (60–65m) make this
  ground brutal for bowlers at the death.

**How the pitch interacts with tonight's team compositions:**
- RCB's batting (Kohli, Salt, Tim David) is ideally suited — power
  hitters thrive on true bounce and short boundaries.
- SRH's ultra-aggressive batting (Head, Abhishek, Klaasen) is equally
  suited. This pitch neutralises bowling quality and amplifies batting.
- The team with the better death bowling, not the better spinners, will
  likely control the outcome.

**Historical averages at this venue:**
- Average first innings score: 175–185 (but 190+ is the modern IPL norm
  here on flat tracks)
- Highest score: SRH 287/3 (IPL 2024) — at this very ground vs RCB
- Lowest score: RCB 82 all out (historical outlier)
- Last IPL match here: RCB 213/5 beat CSK 211/5 by 2 runs (IPL 2025,
  Match 52)

**Expected par score tonight: 190–200**
(Flat pitch + short boundaries + fast outfield + dew for chasers)

## Toss and Conditions

### Toss result

**RCB won the toss and elected to BOWL FIRST.**

This is the expected decision at Chinnaswamy. Chasing is favoured here
(~55% chase success rate historically) and dew in the second innings
makes bowling harder. Patidar's decision aligns with venue norms.

### Historical toss patterns at Chinnaswamy

- Toss winner chooses to field first ~80% of the time in recent IPL
  seasons at this venue.
- Toss advantage: **Moderate.** Winning the toss and fielding first
  provides a meaningful but not decisive edge (~55% win rate for toss
  winner). The advantage is dew-driven, not pitch-driven.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** Expected around 8:00–8:30 PM.
- **Impact tonight:** SIGNIFICANT. This is March (early season), so dew
  will be moderate rather than the heavy May dew, but it will still
  affect second-innings bowling. Spinners will lose grip. Cutters and
  slower balls become harder to execute.
- **Prediction implication:** The team batting second (RCB) has a
  structural advantage from both the toss decision and the dew. Adjust
  par score downward by ~10 runs for the chasing team — a first-innings
  score of 185 plays like 175 for the team chasing under dew.

### Match timing

- Day-night match. First innings under fading light, second innings
  fully under lights.
- Dew will primarily affect the second innings (overs 25–40 overall).

## Venue Form

### Last IPL match at Chinnaswamy

**RCB 213/5 vs CSK 211/5 — RCB won by 2 runs (IPL 2025, Match 52)**
- High-scoring, tight finish — exactly what Chinnaswamy produces.
- RCB batted first and posted 213: Kohli 62, Bethell 55, Shepherd 53*.
- CSK nearly chased it down: Ayush Mhatre 94.
- Confirms: 200+ totals are normal here. Chases are viable even
  against big totals.

### Important note

The stadium was closed for approximately 10 months for safety upgrades
and has just been cleared for IPL 2026. This is the first match back.
The pitch has been freshly prepared — **expect it to play true and flat**
with no wear-and-tear from previous matches.

### Venue scoring trend (recent IPL seasons)

Chinnaswamy has consistently been one of the top 2–3 highest-scoring
IPL venues. The altitude (~920m), short straight boundaries, and fast
outfield all contribute. This is a ground where 180 can feel
under-par.
