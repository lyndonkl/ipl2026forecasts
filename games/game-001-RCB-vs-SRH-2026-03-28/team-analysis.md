## RCB — Batting Unit

### Opening Pair: Virat Kohli + Phil Salt

**Virat Kohli (RHB)**
- IPL 2025: 657 runs, avg 54.75, SR ~135
- IPL 2024: Orange Cap winner (741 runs, avg 61.75)
- Powerplay approach: Typically takes 5–10 balls to settle, then accelerates.
  Not a powerplay basher — his value is anchoring through the middle overs
  and providing the platform for finishers.
- Weakness: Can be slow against quality pace in the first 2 overs. Has
  historically struggled against high-quality wrist spin (but flat pitch
  reduces this risk tonight).
- Chinnaswamy record: Outstanding. His home ground. Averages 40+ here
  in IPL with SR ~140.

**Phil Salt (RHB, wk)**
- Career IPL SR: 175.71 — one of the most destructive openers in world T20s
- Powerplay specialist: Attacks from ball one. Will target the fielding
  restrictions aggressively.
- Weakness: Can be dismissed early if bowlers find swing (new ball, overcast).
  Tonight's conditions (clear skies, flat pitch) suit Salt perfectly.
- IPL 2025: Solid debut IPL season with RCB.

**Opening pair assessment:** Explosive potential. Salt provides the aggression,
Kohli provides the anchor. On a flat Chinnaswamy pitch, this is a top-3
opening pair in IPL 2026. Expected powerplay score: 50–60 if both survive.

### Top Order (3–4): Patidar + Jitesh Sharma

**Rajat Patidar (c, RHB)**
- Role: No.3 — bridges powerplay aggression and death acceleration
- IPL 2025: Key contributor in the title-winning campaign. Aggressive
  stroke-maker, especially strong against spin.
- Captaincy tonight: First match as full-time RCB captain. Added pressure
  but Patidar has shown composure in big games (2022 playoff 112* vs LSG).
- Chinnaswamy: Comfortable here. True bounce suits his front-foot driving.

**Jitesh Sharma (RHB, wk)**
- Role: No.4 — flexible. Can accumulate or accelerate depending on situation.
- IPL career: SR ~155 — capable of explosive innings.
- A slightly surprising selection at No.4 over Bethell, suggesting RCB want
  an Indian middle-order option to maximise overseas slots in bowling.

### Middle Order / Finishers (5–7): Tim David, Shepherd, Krunal

**Tim David (RHB) — FINISHER**
- IPL 2025: 630 runs in death overs at SR 195 — elite finisher numbers.
- Role: Comes in at 5–6, targets death overs. One of the most dangerous
  power hitters in world T20 cricket.
- Chinnaswamy fit: PERFECT. Short boundaries + true bounce = Tim David's
  ideal conditions. Expect 20–40 off 10–20 balls if he gets time.
- Weakness: Can struggle if brought in too early (overs 8–12) against quality
  spin. Best in overs 15–20.

**Romario Shepherd (RHB/RF)**
- Lower-order power hitter + death bowler. Can clear the ropes.
- Batting depth indicator: RCB can bat aggressively to No.8.

**Krunal Pandya (LHB/SLA)**
- Useful lower-order batting. Can rotate strike and hit the occasional six.
- Not a match-winning batter but adds depth.

### Batting Depth Assessment
RCB bat deep to No.8 (Bhuvneshwar at 8 is a tailender). The top 6 are all
capable of match-winning innings. Tim David at No.5 as a specialist finisher
is a luxury. On a flat Chinnaswamy pitch, this batting lineup should
comfortably chase 190+ if they don't lose more than 3 wickets in the
powerplay and middle overs.

## SRH — Batting Unit

### Opening Pair: Abhishek Sharma + Travis Head

**Abhishek Sharma (LHB)**
- IPL powerplay SR: 202.95 — among the highest in IPL history
- Style: Ultra-aggressive from ball one. Hits boundaries to all parts of the
  ground. Left-handed, which creates challenges for right-arm bowlers bowling
  to his strengths (leg side).
- Weakness: HIGH RISK. Will either score 40(18) or 5(4). Inconsistent but
  match-changing when it comes off.
- Against RCB's bowling tonight: Bhuvneshwar's swing could trouble him early.
  Duffy's cutters may also test him. But on a flat pitch, expect aggression.

**Travis Head (LHB)**
- IPL 2024: SR 191.55 — was among the most destructive batters in the
  tournament. SRH's batting identity was built around Head's aggression.
- Style: Similar to Abhishek — all-out attack. Powerful on both sides of
  the wicket. Exceptional against pace — takes on fast bowlers aggressively.
- Weakness: Can be contained by accurate spin in the middle overs. Left-arm
  orthodox can be effective (Krunal tonight).
- Chinnaswamy factor: Short boundaries suit Head's power hitting. Expect
  fireworks if he survives the first 10 balls.

**Opening pair assessment:** EXTREMELY aggressive and EXTREMELY volatile.
Both are left-handed, which is a notable structural issue (see bowling
matchup implications below). This pair will either put up 60(30) in the
powerplay or both be back in the dugout by over 4. The variance is enormous.
Expected powerplay: 55–65 if it clicks, 25–35 if it doesn't.

### Top Order (3): Ishan Kishan (c)

**Ishan Kishan (LHB, wk)**
- Left-handed — making SRH's top 3 ALL left-handed batters. This is a
  significant structural vulnerability that right-arm off-spinners and
  left-arm seamers can exploit.
- Captaining for the first time in IPL — added pressure.
- IPL career: Capable of explosive 60s and 70s but inconsistent. SR ~135.
- Role tonight: Stabiliser if the openers fall early. Must provide the

### Middle Order (4–6): Klaasen, Aniket Verma, Nitish Kumar Reddy

**Heinrich Klaasen (RHB) — FINISHER**
- Career IPL SR: 178.4 — elite finisher.
- IPL 2024: 479 runs at SR 171. Devastating against spin in the death overs.
  One of the best six-hitters in T20 cricket.
- Role: No.4 tonight. Will look to bat through the middle overs and
  unleash in the death. Klaasen vs Krunal Pandya / Suyash Sharma in the
  death overs is a key matchup.
- Weakness: Can be tied down by accurate pace early in his innings. Struggles
  more against pace than spin.

**Aniket Verma**
- Uncapped / relatively unknown. A significant wildcard.
- Limited data makes matchup analysis unreliable. Treat as a neutral factor
  in the prediction — could contribute 15(12) or 0(3).

**Nitish Kumar Reddy (RHB/RMF)**
- Emerging all-rounder. Batted well in IPL 2024 at No.5/6.
- Provides right-handed balance to the otherwise left-heavy top order.
- Can hit long but still developing consistency.

### Batting Depth Assessment
SRH bat to No.7 at best (Salil Arora). After Klaasen and Nitish, the
batting drops off sharply — Salil Arora, Harsh Dubey, Harshal Patel are
not reliable run-scorers. The three uncapped/unknown players (Aniket Verma,
Salil Arora, Harsh Dubey) create significant uncertainty. If SRH lose
4 wickets in the first 12 overs, they may struggle to post 170.

**Key structural issue:** Top 3 ALL left-handed (Abhishek, Head, Ishan).
This allows RCB to set fields and bowling plans for left-handers almost
exclusively in the powerplay and early middle overs.

## RCB — Bowling Unit

### Powerplay Bowlers (Overs 1–6)

**Bhuvneshwar Kumar (right-arm swing, 130–135 kph)**
- Veteran swing bowler. IPL economy in powerplay: historically ~7.0–7.5.
- Still capable of moving the new ball, but pace has declined with age (36+).
- Tonight: Will bowl overs 1–3. Swing may be limited on a flat Chinnaswamy
  pitch, but his accuracy and cutters give him a base level of control.
- Matchup vs SRH openers: Bhuvneshwar's swing could trouble Abhishek early.
  Head is less vulnerable to swing — attacks pace on his pads.

**Jacob Duffy (right-arm fast, 130–140 kph)**
- NZ No.1 T20I bowler. Avg 17.98, econ 7.48 in T20Is.
- Style: Not express pace but smart — cutters, slower balls, good yorker.
- IPL debut tonight. Unknown factor — SRH batters have no data on him.
  This unfamiliarity could work in Duffy's favour.
- Risk: Chinnaswamy's flat pitch and short boundaries are unforgiving for
  any bowler lacking express pace.

**Romario Shepherd (right-arm fast, 135–140 kph)**
- May bowl 1–2 overs in the powerplay. Hits the deck hard, gets bounce.
- Useful as a change bowler but not a primary new-ball option.

### Middle Overs Bowlers (Overs 7–15)

**Krunal Pandya (left-arm orthodox spin)**
- Primary spin option. Economy-focused — not a big wicket-taker.
- Tonight's matchup is CRITICAL: SRH's top 3 are ALL left-handed. Left-arm
  orthodox bowler to left-handed batters spins AWAY from them — creates
  the outside edge and stumping opportunities.
- Krunal vs Abhishek/Head/Ishan could be the middle-overs battle that
  shapes this game.
- Flat pitch modifier: Spin will turn minimally. Krunal must rely on flight,
  accuracy, and changes of pace rather than turn.

**Suyash Sharma (leg-spin)**
- Young leg-spinner. Will bowl 2–4 overs in the middle phase.
- Against SRH's left-handers: leg-spin turns INTO left-handers — less
  threatening than off-spin or left-arm orthodox. But the googly could be
  effective.
- Against Klaasen (RHB): Klaasen murders leg-spin. This is a high-risk
  matchup for Suyash.

### Death Over Specialists (Overs 16–20)

**Romario Shepherd (primary death bowler)**
- Hits hard lengths, bowls yorkers, has a good slower ball.
- Expected to bowl overs 17 and 19.
- Chinnaswamy death bowling is a brutal assignment — short boundaries
  mean execution must be perfect.

**Bhuvneshwar Kumar (secondary death option)**
- Experienced death bowler but declining effectiveness at 36+.
- Will likely bowl over 18 or 20.
- Dew will make his cutters and slower balls harder to execute (RCB are
  bowling first, so dew is less relevant for their bowling — this is
  the first innings).

**Jacob Duffy (may bowl at the death)**
- Death bowling credentials unknown at IPL level. Risk factor.

### Overall Bowling Balance

**Pace-spin ratio:** 3 pace + 1 AR pace + 2 spin = pace-heavy.
**Weak link:** Abhinandan Singh. If he bowls, SRH will likely target his
overs. An unknown medium-pacer at Chinnaswamy is a recipe for punishment.
**Without Hazlewood:** RCB's bowling lacks a genuine X-factor pace bowler.
Bhuvneshwar is smart but aging, Duffy is capable but unproven in IPL,
Shepherd is an all-rounder not a specialist. This bowling attack may
struggle to defend a total but is adequate for first-innings restriction.

of off-spin on a flat Chinnaswamy pitch against RCB's right-hand-heavy
lineup is a significant vulnerability.
**Strongest asset:** Harshal Patel at the death. If SRH can get through

gives them a genuine chance of restricting RCB in overs 16–20.
**Without Cummins:** The bowling attack drops from competitive to below
average. The gap between SRH's bowling tonight and their full-strength
