# Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium, Mullanpur (New Chandigarh)

### Games at this venue

## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~38,000
- **Home team:** Punjab Kings (since IPL 2024)
- **Pitch type:** Balanced — good pace and bounce, batting-friendly with steady carry
- **Pitch composition:** Red soil with natural grass, sand-based outfield (not conventional soil)
- **Boundaries:** Square 64-69m (short — high six count), straight 70-75m
- **Outfield:** Super-fast Bermuda grass
- **Drainage:** Herringbone system — clears water in 25-30 minutes
- **IPL matches hosted:** ~11 (IPL 2024-2025)

## Key Characteristics
- Balanced surface: good for batting throughout but offers enough for quality pace early
- **Pace bowling dominates:** 62% of overs bowled by pace, 142 wickets vs 51 for spin (nearly 3:1 ratio)
- Short square boundaries (64-69m) encourage six-hitting — 121 sixes in 8 matches
- Pitch can become slower/uneven as match progresses — spinners gain grip in middle/late overs
- Sand-based surface is more stable but harder to maintain than traditional pitches
- **Bat-first advantage:** Teams batting first won 5 of 10 completed IPL games (50%) — was 55% pre-Game 004, now even after field-first won Game 004
- Has produced both 228/5 (highest) and 95 all out (lowest) — wide scoring range
- PBKS have a mixed home record here — not a fortress

## Scoring Benchmarks
- **Average first innings score:** ~170 (historical avg 169.37 across 8 IPL matches)
- **Average second innings score:** ~157 (historical avg 156.62)
- **Powerplay average:** ~7.28 RPO (43-50 runs in 6 overs)
- **Death overs:** High variance — 50-70 runs in final 5 overs depending on dew/conditions
- **Score range (all IPL games):** 95–228
- **Par score:** 170-180 (scores 180+ have strong success rate; 200+ rarely chased)
- **Highest:** 228/5 (MI vs GT, IPL 2025 Eliminator)
- **Lowest:** 95 all out (KKR vs PBKS, April 2025) — PBKS defended 111 (lowest defended in IPL history)

## Toss and Conditions
- **Bat first vs chase:** 5-4 in favour of bat first (55%) — one of few IPL venues favouring batting first
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate — evening matches favour bowling first (dew), but historical data shows bat-first edge
- **Dew factor:** Onset typically after 9:00 PM IST. Moderate to significant. Ball skids on, spinners lose grip. Death overs especially affected.
- **Climate:** Semi-dry, low humidity (16-29%). Dew is the primary moisture factor, not atmospheric humidity.
- **Day/afternoon games:** Minimal dew interference. Batting first even more advantageous.
- **Wind:** Typically light (8-12 km/h), minimal match impact.
