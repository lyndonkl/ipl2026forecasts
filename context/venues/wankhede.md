## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~33,000
- **Home team:** Mumbai Indians
- **Pitch type:** Flat batting surface with good pace and carry
- **Pitch composition:** Red soil — consistent bounce, ball comes nicely onto the bat
- **Boundaries:** Short square boundaries — extremely challenging for death bowlers

## Key Characteristics
- Classic batting-friendly venue — pace-friendly, spin-unfriendly
- Pitch flattens further under lights in second innings
- Coastal humidity can assist early swing (first 2-3 overs only)
- Spin gets minimal assistance — Wankhede is NOT a spin-friendly venue
- Short square boundaries make death-over bowling the most important skill
- Favours teams with strong pace attacks and explosive batting

## Scoring Benchmarks
- **Average first innings score:** 170–175 (historical)
- **IPL 2026 benchmark:** 220 (Game 002 — both teams scored 220+, suggesting pitch was dead flat)
- **Par score:** 175–185 first innings (may need upward revision after Game 002)
- **Below 165:** Below par
- **200+:** Strong but chaseable (MI chased 220 with 5 balls to spare)

## Toss and Conditions
- **Chase win rate:** ~56–58% (chasing favoured)
- Toss advantage: Moderate — chasing preferred
- Advantage from chasing known target + pitch flattening under lights
- **Dew factor (late March):** MINIMAL. Mumbai is hot and dry in March. Dew becomes significant from mid-April onward
- Wind: Can gust to 39 km/h — affects high catches and ball flight over short boundaries

## Bowler/Batter Profiles That Succeed
- **Batters:** Aggressive stroke-makers thrive. True bounce means batters can hit through the line at all phases. Pace onto the bat suits wristwork
- **Pace bowlers:** Early swing possible (2-3 overs with new ball). Death bowling execution is critical — yorkers and variations essential
- **Spinners:** Minimal turn. Mystery spinners with pace variation can survive but won't dominate. Varun Chakravarthy went 0/48 in 4 overs in Game 002
- **Key lesson from Game 002:** Do NOT underestimate this surface. 170-175 historical average may be outdated — modern IPL batting pushed both teams to 220+
