## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~50,000
- **Home team:** Lucknow Super Giants (LSG)
- **Pitch type:** Variable — balanced to seam-friendly early season; can become slow and low on used pitches
- **Pitch composition:** Mixed black and red soil. Black soil provides grip for spinners as it wears; red soil gives pace and carry on fresh pitches
- **Boundaries:** Longer side — large outfield. Not a small-boundary ground
- **Outfield:** Large, rewards ground fielding and running between wickets

## Key Characteristics
- One of the most unpredictable IPL venues — can produce 200-run surfaces or 130-all-out collapses in the same season
- Fresh pitches tend to be balanced with decent pace and carry; used pitches trend slow and low or turn
- Dew becomes a factor from ~8:00–8:30 PM onward — moderate severity
- Chasing favoured: ~55% chase success rate historically
- Toss winner typically bowls first (~70% of the time in recent IPL seasons)
- Not an extreme spin or seam venue — balanced conditions reward well-rounded attacks

## Scoring Benchmarks
- **Average first innings score:** 160–175 (venue base rate); trended higher in IPL 2025 (~187)
- **Par score:** 175–185 on a fresh pitch; 155–165 on a used/slow pitch
- **Below 165:** Under-par on a fresh pitch
- **200+:** Achievable but not routine — happened in IPL 2025 (LSG vs MI: 208/5)
- **Highest (recent IPL):** 208/5 (LSG vs MI, IPL 2025)
- **Lowest (recent IPL):** ~130 (when bowling dominated on used surface)

## Toss and Conditions
- Toss winner advantage: Moderate (~55% win rate)
- Advantage is dew-driven, not pitch-driven
- Dew onset: ~8:00–8:30 PM. Moderate severity — spinners lose grip, fast bowlers struggle with yorker accuracy
- Historical bat-first win %: ~40% (from IPL 2025 data)
- Historical chase win %: ~55%

## Dew Profile
- **Onset:** 8:00–8:30 PM (approximately overs 10–13 of the second innings in evening matches)
- **Severity:** Moderate — humidity rises after 9 PM
- **Impact:** Significant on spinners (lose grip and turn); noticeable on pace (slippery ball reduces control)
- **Structural advantage:** Chasing team gains ~8–10% win probability boost from dew

## Bowler/Batter Profiles That Succeed
- **Batters:** Versatile batters who can adjust to variable surfaces. On fresh pitches, stroke-makers thrive. On used pitches, spin-hitters and sweep players are valuable
- **Bowlers:** Medium-fast bowlers with good variations (cutters, slower balls) are effective across pitch types. Hit-the-deck pace bowlers on fresh pitches; finger spinners on worn surfaces
- **Spinners:** Moderate assistance. More effective on reused pitches. Wrist spinners with the googly can be dangerous when the pitch grips
