# M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bengaluru

### Games at this venue
- [Game 001: RCB vs SRH (Mar 28)](../../games/game-001-RCB-vs-SRH-2026-03-28/outcome.md)

## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~40,000
- **Altitude:** ~920m (contributes to ball carrying further)
- **Home team:** RCB
- **Pitch type:** Flat batting surface, true bounce, fast outfield
- **Boundaries:** Short straight boundaries (60–65m) — among smallest in IPL
- **Pitch composition:** Consistent bounce, ball comes on to the bat at pace

## Key Characteristics
- Consistently one of the top 2–3 highest-scoring IPL venues
- Spin gets minimal turn — spinners must rely on flight, pace changes, accuracy
- Death bowling execution decides close games (short boundaries punish errors)
- Dew becomes a factor from ~8:00 PM onward — moderate in early season (March), heavier in May
- Chasing favoured: ~55% chase success rate historically
- Toss winner fields first ~80% of the time in recent seasons

## Scoring Benchmarks
- **Average first innings score:** 175–185 (190+ is modern IPL norm here)
- **Par score:** 190–200 first innings
- **Below 175:** Under-par
- **200+:** Competitive but chaseable
- **Highest:** SRH 287/3 (IPL 2024 vs RCB)
- **Lowest:** RCB 82 all out (historical outlier)

## Toss and Conditions
- Toss winner advantage: Moderate (~55% win rate)
- Advantage is dew-driven, not pitch-driven
- Dew onset: ~8:00–8:30 PM. Spinners lose grip, cutters harder to execute

## Bowler/Batter Profiles That Succeed
- **Batters:** Power hitters thrive (true bounce + short boundaries). Both aggressive and anchor approaches work
- **Bowlers:** Death bowling specialists most valuable. Pace > spin on this surface. Early swing lasts only 2-3 overs
- **Spinners:** Minimal assistance. Not a spin-friendly venue

## Special Notes (IPL 2026)
- Stadium reopened after ~10 months of safety upgrades
- First match back on freshly prepared pitch — played true and flat as expected
