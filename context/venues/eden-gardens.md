## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~68,000
- **Home team:** Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Pitch type:** Balanced to slightly slow; batting-dominant surface with good bounce and carry
- **Pitch composition:** True surface — ball comes onto the bat. Gradual slowdown through middle and death overs. Modest spin assistance, not dramatic.
- **Boundaries:** Large outfield (~75m) — run-saving fielding at a premium. Not a small-ground slogfest like Wankhede.

## Key Characteristics
- Batting-dominant venue with a balanced, true pitch
- Good bounce and carry in the powerplay aids strokeplay
- Pitch slows gradually through the innings — "hold" rather than "spin" in the death overs
- Spin gets modest assistance — mystery spinners (Narine, Varun) have historically leveraged this at home, but it's not a turning track
- Large outfield makes death bowling slightly more forgiving than small-ground venues — boundaries require genuine timing
- T20 World Cup semi-final (NZ vs SA) played on this pitch confirmed high-scoring, competitive surface
- Favours teams with strong middle-order rotation and death-over execution

## Scoring Benchmarks
- **Average first innings score:** 165–175 (historical IPL)
- **Average second innings score:** ~140 (reflects dew impact on bowling, not pitch deterioration)
- **Par score:** 170–180 first innings
- **Below 160:** Under par — vulnerable total
- **180+:** Strong total, though still chaseable with dew assistance
- **200+:** Commanding — requires something special to chase

## Toss and Conditions
- **Bat first win rate:** ~48%
- **Chase win rate:** ~52%
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate — chasing preferred
- Toss winner typically elects to bowl first (secure drier batting conditions for the chase)
- Chase advantage comes from known target + dew assistance + pitch remaining true
- **Dew factor (April):** HEAVY. Onset ~7:30–8:00 PM (as early as 7:45 PM with high humidity). Dew progressively intensifies through the second innings as temperatures drop from ~30°C to ~25°C.
- Dew impact: Spinners lose grip significantly; pacers lose reverse swing; fielding becomes hazardous (wet ball, misfields); batting easier as ball skids onto bat
- Humidity: 65–75% typical for April evening matches — accelerates dew formation
- Wind: Light, typically north-easterly breeze. Not a major factor.

## Bowler/Batter Profiles That Succeed
- **Batters:** Stroke-makers who play through the line thrive on the true bounce. Middle-order rotators who manipulate the large outfield are valuable. Power hitters need genuine timing — the large boundaries don't gift sixes like Wankhede or Chinnaswamy.
- **Pace bowlers:** Good bounce and carry aids new-ball bowlers. Death bowling execution (yorkers, cutters, slower balls) is critical, especially in dew. Conventional seam more reliable than reverse swing.
- **Spinners:** Modest turn — mystery spinners with pace variation can succeed but won't dominate. Dew from overs 12-13 significantly reduces spin effectiveness in the second innings. First-innings spin is more viable.
- **Death specialists:** Bowlers who execute dew-prepared variations (cutters, knuckle balls, wide yorkers) outperform those relying on grip-dependent deliveries (leg-spin, conventional slower balls).
