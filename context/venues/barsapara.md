## Venue Profile
- **Capacity:** ~40,000
- **Home team:** Rajasthan Royals (IPL home games)
- **Pitch type:** Batting-friendly, true surface
- **Boundaries:** Standard size
- **Location:** Northeast India — humid, potential for dew in evening games

## Key Characteristics
- Good for batting throughout — not a dead flat road like Wankhede but consistently high-scoring
- Scores cluster 150-200 (narrower range than Chinnaswamy/Wankhede)
- **Leg-spin has been devastating at this venue** — the single most important bowling pattern
- Left-arm pace effective early (Boult 3/28 in 2023)
- Death bowling specialists decide close games
- Pure pace without variation has been less effective
- RR's "home" ground but they've actually lost 3 of 5 completed IPL games here — NOT a fortress

- **Average first innings score:** ~180 (8.56 runs/over)
- **Powerplay average:** 9.16–9.41 RPO (55-57 runs) — above league average
- **Death overs average:** 9.92 RPO (~50 runs in last 5 overs)
- **Score range (last 6 IPL games):** 127–199 (most cluster 150-200, but sub-130 possible with bowling dominance)
- **Highest:** 199/4 (RR vs DC, 2023)
- **Lowest:** 127 all out (CSK vs RR, 2026) — previously 142/9 (DC vs RR, 2023)

## Toss and Conditions
- **Bat first vs chase:** 2-2 from completed games — no clear advantage
- Toss advantage: Not decisive at this venue
- **Dew factor:** Expected in second innings (evening games, 7:30 PM start). Favours chasing slightly
- **Weather (March):** 24-27°C, humid, slight shower risk but evenings usually playable

- Pattern: When RR's bowling clicks, they dominate (199 vs DC, bowled them out for 142). When it doesn't, other teams chase comfortably
