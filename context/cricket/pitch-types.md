# IPL Pitch Types — Tactical Reference

This file defines the five main IPL pitch archetypes and their
implications for prediction. Reference this when writing
conditions-report.md for any match.

---

## 1. Flat Batting Surface

**Typical venues:** Chinnaswamy (Bengaluru), Wankhede (Mumbai),
Narendra Modi Stadium (Ahmedabad — centre wicket).

### Behaviour by phase

- **Overs 1–6:** True bounce, ball comes onto the bat, little lateral
  movement. New ball carries to boundary. Openers play through the line.
- **Overs 7–15:** Surface remains consistent. Spinners get limited turn
  and operate as containing options, not wicket-taking threats. Batters
  hit through the line with confidence.
- **Overs 16–20:** Pitch does not deteriorate. Death bowling execution
  is decisive — yorkers and slower balls matter more than the surface.
  Totals of 190+ common; 200+ achievable.

### Bowling effectiveness

- **Pace:** Moderate. Express pace (145+ kph) extracts bounce but length
  bowlers get punished. Back-of-length is the danger zone.
- **Swing:** Minimal after the first 2–3 overs unless overcast.
- **Spin:** Least effective surface. Leg-spinners relying on turn are
  neutered. Finger spinners must rely on accuracy and pace changes.

### Batting advantage

- Stroke-makers and power hitters at maximum advantage. Anchor-style
  batters at SR 110–120 are a liability — the surface demands aggression.

### Tactical adjustments

- Bat first: target 185–200+. Below 170 is under-par.
- Bowling teams prioritise powerplay wickets over middle-over containment.
- Best death bowler bowls at both ends in overs 17–20.

### Scoring benchmarks

- Average first innings: 175–190 | Competitive: 180+ | Par chase: 175
- Sub-150 is a poor batting effort on a true flat track.

## 2. Seaming Green Top

**Typical venues:** Dharamsala (early season), Chepauk (fresh pitch
with grass coverage), Lucknow (occasionally).

### Behaviour by phase

- **Overs 1–6:** Significant lateral seam movement. Ball nips both ways
  off a good length. Green tinge = moisture = grip and deviation. This
  is the most dangerous phase for batting.
- **Overs 7–15:** Seam movement diminishes as surface dries and ball
  loses shine. Batting becomes progressively easier. Spinners still get
  limited turn — moisture suppresses spin.
- **Overs 16–20:** Usually flatter by now. Death overs can play closer
  to a flat surface once moisture has evaporated.

### Bowling effectiveness

- **Pace (seam):** Maximum effectiveness in the powerplay. Hit-the-deck
  bowlers at good length (135–145 kph) are most dangerous.
- **Swing:** Amplified by cloud cover and humidity. Swing bowlers with
  the new ball are at peak advantage.
- **Spin:** Below average early. May improve slightly as surface dries
  in the second innings.

### Batting advantage

- Batters with strong technique against seam — play late, leave well,
  solid defence. Top-order anchors with Test-match pedigree thrive.
- Front-foot drivers are vulnerable to movement off the pitch.

### Tactical adjustments

- Toss winner strongly prefers to bowl first.
- Batting teams may promote a technically solid batter over an aggressive
  dasher to open.
- Three quality seamers minimum; a fourth seam option is valuable.
- First innings 155–170 is competitive.

### Scoring benchmarks

- Average first innings: 155–170 | Competitive: 160+ | Par chase: 155
- Sub-130 collapses more common than on any other surface type.

## 3. Dry Turning Track

**Typical venues:** Chepauk (Chennai — used pitch, second/third match),
Lucknow (later in tournament), Ahmedabad (worn pitch), Raipur.

### Behaviour by phase

- **Overs 1–6:** May play relatively normally. Some pitches turn from
  ball one; others only begin turning after overs 8–10. New ball skids
  through — spinners not immediately effective.
- **Overs 7–15:** Pitch comes alive for spin. Rough patches develop
  outside off stump for off-spinners and outside leg for leg-spinners.
  Spin is the primary wicket-taking weapon.
- **Overs 16–20:** Maximum turn and variable bounce. Some balls grip
  and turn sharply; others skid through low. Death-over scoring is
  extremely difficult. Run rates drop significantly.

### Bowling effectiveness

- **Pace:** Reduced. Ball skids at low-to-medium pace. Cutters and
  off-cutters from medium-pacers grip effectively.
- **Spin (finger):** Off-spinners and left-arm orthodox at maximum
  effectiveness. Exploit footmarks and rough. Length balls turning away
  from right-handers are the money delivery.
- **Spin (wrist):** Leg-spinners with consistent googly are devastating.
  Turn is exaggerated. But inaccurate wrist spinners get swept.

### Batting advantage

- Batters who play spin well — using feet, sweeping, reverse-sweeping.
  Left-handers who disrupt the off-spinner's line are valuable.
- Power hitters relying on pace on the ball struggle.

### Tactical adjustments

- Team with the better spin attack usually wins. 2–3 quality spinners
  is a structural advantage.
- Batting first often preferred — pitch deteriorates further.
- First innings 165–175 competitive; 180+ often match-winning.
- Impact sub decisions often revolve around an extra spinner.

### Scoring benchmarks

- Average first innings: 160–175 | Competitive: 170+ | Par chase: 160
- Sub-120 collapses happen when spin dominates completely.

## 4. Slow and Low

**Typical venues:** Chepauk (late-season used pitch), Lucknow (some
matches), Delhi (when pitch is under-prepared or used repeatedly).

### Behaviour by phase

- **Overs 1–6:** Ball does not come onto the bat. Drives die in front
  of fielders. Pull shots mistimed because bounce is lower than expected.
- **Overs 7–15:** Scoring difficult across all styles. Ball grips and
  holds in the surface. Neither pace nor spin is easy to score off. Run
  rates drop to 6.5–7.5 RPO.
- **Overs 16–20:** Very difficult to score at 10+ RPO. Ball stops in
  the pitch. Power hitters cannot generate bat speed. Cutters, slower
  balls, and knuckle balls extremely effective.

### Bowling effectiveness

- **Pace:** Medium-fast (130–140 kph) with good variations are more
  effective than express pace. Slower balls, cutters, off-pace deliveries
  are the key weapons.
- **Spin:** Effective for containment. Turn may be modest but lack of
  pace off the pitch makes scoring hard. Stump-to-stump with subtle
  variations dominates.

### Batting advantage

- Batters who rotate strike and manufacture runs — nudges, deflections,
  reverse sweeps. Classical stroke players relying on timing struggle.
- Wrist strength matters for generating power despite slow surface.

### Tactical adjustments

- Totals compressed. 150–160 competitive; 170+ is strong.
- Second-innings surface slows further — chasing harder as the surface
  deteriorates, partially offsetting the usual chase advantage.

### Scoring benchmarks

- Average first innings: 150–165 | Competitive: 155+ | Par chase: 150
- Sub-130 possible even for strong batting lineups.

## 5. Hard and Bouncy

**Typical venues:** HPCA Dharamsala (especially early season),
Lucknow (fresh pitch), Raipur (occasionally).

### Behaviour by phase

- **Overs 1–6:** Steep bounce carries to the keeper. Short balls rise
  sharply — top-edge catches common. Good-length ball arrives chest-high.
  Overpitched deliveries punished because true bounce aids timing.
- **Overs 7–15:** Bounce remains consistent. Spinners who extract bounce
  (tall off-spinners, overspin leg-spinners) more effective. Spinners
  relying on turn without bounce struggle.
- **Overs 16–20:** Bounce persists. Short-ball plans viable at the
  death — rare on most IPL surfaces. Bouncers and back-of-length at
  140+ are genuine wicket-taking options.

### Bowling effectiveness

- **Pace (short):** Most effective on this surface. Bowlers hitting
  short-of-a-length corridor at 140+ extract steep bounce and top-edge
  chances.
- **Pace (full):** Risky — true bounce makes full balls easier to drive.
- **Spin:** Variable. Bounce helps spinners who flight and use dip. Flat,
  quick spinners are ineffective without turn to assist.

### Batting advantage

- Batters comfortable against the short ball — pull, hook, ride the
  bounce and cut — thrive.
- Front-foot dominant batters who play across the line are vulnerable.

### Tactical adjustments

- Short-ball plans viable throughout the innings, not just in powerplay.
- Tall, bouncy fast bowlers (190+ cm, 140+ kph) have structural advantage.
- Catching becomes important — top edges and skied pulls create chances.

### Scoring benchmarks

- Average first innings: 165–180 | Competitive: 175+ | Par chase: 165
- Sub-140 when short-ball plans work consistently.

## Identifying Pitch Type from Pre-Match Reports

### Visual clues

| Clue | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| Green tinge, grass visible | Seaming green top |
| Brown/dusty, bare patches | Dry turning track |
| Grey, hard, shiny surface | Hard and bouncy |
| Pale, worn, visible cracks | Slow and low |
| White/beige, rolled flat | Flat batting surface |

### Curator language decoder

- "We've left some grass for pace" → Seaming
- "Good batting surface, true bounce" → Flat
- "Should take turn as the game progresses" → Dry turning
- "It's a used pitch, played on yesterday" → Slow and low / turning
- "Hard surface with good carry" → Hard and bouncy

### Match number on the pitch

- First match on a fresh strip: favours pace
- Second match on same strip: more variable, may turn
- Third match on same strip: slow and low / turning
