## 1. M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bengaluru

**Home team:** Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)
**Capacity:** ~40,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 175–185
- **Typical pitch type:** Flat batting surface. True bounce, even carry.
  Occasionally offers some assistance for pace in the first 3–4 overs.
- **High-scoring ground:** One of the highest-scoring venues in IPL
  history. 200+ totals are common.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~45% (chasing slightly favoured)
- **Chase success rate:** ~55%
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate — toss winner typically fields first.
  Not decisively toss-dependent.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** 8:00–8:30 PM. Moderate to heavy dew in April–May.
- **Impact:** Significant — second-innings bowling becomes harder.
  Spinners lose grip. Enhances the chase advantage.

### Ground quirks

- Short straight boundaries (~60–65m). Favours batters who hit down
  the ground. Six-hitting is easier than at most venues.
- Altitude (~920m) means the ball travels further through the air.
- Can be a nightmare for bowlers — totals of 220+ have been posted here.

## 2. Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai

**Home team:** Mumbai Indians (MI)
**Capacity:** ~33,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 170–180
- **Typical pitch type:** Flat to hard and bouncy. Good pace and carry.
  Suited to stroke-play. Offers something for fast bowlers early.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~43%
- **Chase success rate:** ~57% (one of the strongest chase records)
- **Toss advantage:** Significant — dew makes chasing strongly favourable.
  Toss winner fields first ~80% of the time.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** 7:30–8:00 PM. Heavy dew in April–May (coastal humidity).
- **Impact:** Among the most dew-affected venues in the IPL. Bowling
  second is significantly harder. Spin effectiveness drops sharply.

### Ground quirks

- Coastal sea breeze can assist swing, especially early in the evening.
- Short square boundaries. Favours cross-bat shots — pulls, sweeps.
- Surface can crack under heat, occasionally producing variable bounce
  in late-season matches.

## 3. M.A. Chidambaram Stadium (Chepauk), Chennai

**Home team:** Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
**Capacity:** ~50,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 155–170 (one of the lowest in IPL)
- **Typical pitch type:** Varies significantly match to match. Fresh
  pitch can be seaming. Used pitch becomes dry turning track or slow
  and low. This variability makes Chepauk one of the hardest venues to
  predict.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~52–55% (one of the few IPL venues where
  batting first is advantaged)
- **Chase success rate:** ~45–48%
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate — but the advantage flips depending on
  pitch condition. On a fresh pitch, bowling first is good. On a used
  pitch, batting first is preferred because the surface deteriorates.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** Later than Mumbai/Bangalore — 8:30–9:00 PM.
- **Impact:** Moderate. Dew does arrive but is less heavy than coastal
  venues. Less reliable as a prediction factor.

### Ground quirks

- Spin-friendly reputation is earned — CSK have historically built
  spin-heavy squads to exploit Chepauk's turning conditions.
- Pitch changes dramatically between first and second use. Always check
  how many matches have been played on the pitch.
- Square boundaries are relatively long (~70m). Favours placement over
  power.

## 4. Eden Gardens, Kolkata

**Home team:** Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
**Capacity:** ~68,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 165–175
- **Typical pitch type:** Varies — can be flat or slow depending on
  preparation. Historically has offered assistance for spin in the
  second innings.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~48%
- **Chase success rate:** ~52%
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate. Dew makes fielding first attractive.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** 7:30–8:00 PM. Heavy dew in April–May.
- **Impact:** Significant — similar to Wankhede. Bowling second is hard.

### Ground quirks

- Large outfield — run-saving fielding is at a premium. Boundaries are
  longer than Chinnaswamy or Wankhede.
- Can produce surprisingly low-scoring games when spin grips. KKR have
  historically leveraged mystery spin (Narine, Varun) here effectively.
- Drop-in pitch occasionally used, which can behave differently from
  traditional preparations.

## 5. Arun Jaitley Stadium, Delhi

**Home team:** Delhi Capitals (DC)
**Capacity:** ~41,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 165–180
- **Typical pitch type:** Variable — can be flat early in the season
  and slow and low when pitches are reused. Has produced both 200+ and
  sub-130 scores in the same tournament.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~45%
- **Chase success rate:** ~55%
- **Toss advantage:** Moderate to significant. Dew is a factor.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** 8:00–8:30 PM. Moderate dew.
- **Impact:** Noticeable but less extreme than Wankhede or Eden Gardens.

### Ground quirks

- North Delhi heat in April–May (40°C+) can affect player stamina and
  ball behaviour (reverse swing becomes possible with the old ball).
- Pitch historically has been inconsistent — curator has produced
  different surfaces for different matches in the same tournament.
  Check recent match results at this venue carefully.
- Relatively short boundaries all round. Good for power hitters.

## 7. Sawai Mansingh Stadium, Jaipur

## 10. HPCA Stadium, Dharamsala

**Home team:** Punjab Kings (PBKS) — secondary home venue (3 matches)
**Capacity:** ~23,000

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 165–180
- **Typical pitch type:** Hard and bouncy, especially early season.
  Good carry, steep bounce. Can also offer seam movement in the first
  few overs if grass is left on.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~50% (small sample)
- **Chase success rate:** ~50%
- **Toss advantage:** Low. Conditions are relatively neutral.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** Lower altitude venues in the plains get more dew.
  Dharamsala at ~1,450m altitude has cooler evenings but dew is moderate.
- **Impact:** Less decisive than at plains venues.

### Ground quirks

- Stunning mountain backdrop — but the altitude means the ball travels
  further through the thinner air. Six-hitting is marginally easier.
- Cooler temperatures (especially evening) can help swing bowling.
- Small ground with intimate atmosphere. Short boundaries on some sides.
- Morning moisture can make the new ball swing more than expected.

## 11. Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad

**Home team:** Gujarat Titans (GT)
**Capacity:** ~132,000 (largest cricket stadium in the world)

### Scoring patterns

- **Average first innings score:** 165–180
- **Typical pitch type:** Has varied historically. Can produce flat
  surfaces on the centre wicket and slower, turning surfaces on the
  outer wickets. Curator has multiple pitch blocks available.

### Win rates

- **Batting first win rate:** ~48%
- **Chase success rate:** ~52%
- **Toss advantage:** Low to moderate.

### Dew factor

- **Dew onset:** 8:00–8:30 PM. Moderate dew in April–May.
- **Impact:** Moderate. Gujarat's dry climate means less dew than
  coastal venues.

### Ground quirks

- Enormous outfield. Boundaries can be set very long. This makes it
  harder for batters to hit over the field and rewards bowlers who
  bowl to their fields.
- The sheer size of the ground means running between wickets and
  ground fielding are particularly important.
- Multiple pitch blocks mean consecutive matches can play on
  completely different surfaces.
- Has hosted IPL finals and big knockout games.
