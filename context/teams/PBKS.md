## Team Style

### Batting approach
Iyer brings a structured approach. Stoinis and Omarzai add power in the
middle. Jansen can hit long at 7–8. The batting is built on all-round
depth rather than specialist top-order firepower. This team bats deep
but may lack a genuine elite T20 opener.

### Bowling approach
Arshdeep is one of India's best T20 bowlers — left-arm pace, excellent
death bowling. Chahal provides leg-spin artistry. Jansen gives left-arm
pace at 140+ kph with bounce. Ferguson (when available) adds express
pace (150+ kph). Stoinis and Omarzai provide seam-bowling AR overs.
This is a deep, versatile bowling attack.

---

## Strengths

- **All-round depth:** Stoinis, Jansen, Omarzai, Musheer — multiple
  players who bat and bowl. PBKS can bat to 8–9.
- **Bowling variety:** Left-arm pace (Arshdeep, Jansen), leg-spin
  (Chahal), express pace (Ferguson), right-arm medium (Stoinis/Omarzai).
  Hard to plan against.
- **Ponting coaching:** Elite coach who has improved teams tactically.
- **IPL 2025 final experience:** PBKS reached the final. The squad
  knows how to compete under pressure.

## Vulnerabilities

- **No superstar batter:** Lacks a Kohli/SKY/Jaiswal-calibre opener.
  Iyer's T20 strike rate is often questioned.
- **Overseas slot juggling:** Too many quality overseas options
  (Stoinis, Jansen, Omarzai, Ferguson, Bartlett, Connolly, Owen) —
  only 4 can play. Managing expectations is a challenge.
- **Split home venues:** Mullanpur and Dharamsala are very different.
  Dharamsala is hard and bouncy; Mullanpur is still establishing its
  character.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Dharamsala's bounce suits Jansen and Ferguson. Mullanpur is still new —
treat with lower confidence. Chahal could be effective on dry surfaces
if either venue offers turn.

**Game 004 XI insight (Mullanpur):** PBKS went with 4 overseas: Stoinis, Jansen, Connolly, Bartlett.
Omarzai and Brar were both dropped. Ferguson unavailable. This means PBKS's overseas selection
at Mullanpur prioritizes pace depth (Bartlett) and batting depth (Connolly) over the AR option (Omarzai).

**Chahal at Mullanpur is now a proven pattern:** 4/28 vs KKR (2025) + 4-0-28-2 vs GT (2026). Two
near-identical performances. Treat Chahal at Mullanpur as a base-rate fact in future predictions.
