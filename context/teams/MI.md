## Team Style

### Batting approach
Aggressive top-heavy lineup. Rohit and de Kock aim to dominate the
powerplay. SKY is a 360-degree batter who accelerates through the middle
overs. Tilak Varma provides left-handed anchor with finishing ability.
Hardik is the designated finisher but also bats at 5 to stabilise if
early wickets fall.

### Bowling approach
Bumrah-Boult is one of the strongest new-ball pairings in the IPL.
Bumrah's death bowling is elite (sub-8.0 economy in overs 16–20 career).
Boult provides powerplay swing. Deepak Chahar offers seam and swing.
Spin is the weaker area — Santner or Markande must contain in the
middle overs.

---

## Strengths

- **Bumrah:** Best death bowler in T20 cricket. Single-handedly changes
  games in overs 17–20.
- **Top-order firepower:** Rohit, de Kock, SKY — three of the most
  destructive T20 batters in world cricket.
- **Pace depth:** Bumrah, Boult, Chahar, Shardul gives 4 quality seam
  options.

## Vulnerabilities

- **Spin bowling:** Weakest area. If conditions demand 8+ overs of
  quality spin, MI struggle.
- **Middle-order depth:** If Rohit, de Kock, and SKY all fail, the
  middle order carries heavy responsibility.
- **Hardik's fitness:** Injury history is a persistent risk. If Hardik
  cannot bowl his 4 overs, MI's balance is compromised.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Wankhede's flat, pace-friendly surface suits MI's bowling strength
(Bumrah/Boult) and batting firepower. Heavy dew favours chasing — MI's
batting lineup suits second-innings chases. Spin weakness is less
exposed at Wankhede than at Chepauk or Eden Gardens.
