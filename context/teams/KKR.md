## Game 006 Post-Match Analysis (KKR vs SRH, April 2, 2026)

**Result:** KKR LOST to SRH decisively (159/8 vs target 227)

**Key Findings:**

1. **Pace bowling crisis deeper than expected:**
   - Arora and Muzarabani both ineffective against SRH's LHB opening pair (Head 46, Abhishek 48)
   - Without Harshit Rana, KKR's new-ball bowling is a genuine structural weakness
   - Arora/Muzarabani combination does not provide the strike-bowling threat KKR needs
   - Recommendation: prioritize Harshit Rana recovery timeline

2. **Varun Chakravarthy form concern (sustained, not variance):**
   - Game 2: 0/48 vs MI; Game 6: ineffective vs SRH (226/8 allowed)
   - Pattern suggests sustained form dip, not a one-off. Confidence lost after Game 2?
   - Eden Gardens spin advantage did not materialize
   - Recommendation: monitor Varun's psychological state; may need confidence-building role (bowled in lower-pressure overs, or rest before next match)

3. **Chase collapse in middle overs:**
   - KKR reached 159/8 by overs 15 (target 227)
   - Indicates early wickets + loss of momentum; middle-order batting stability questioned
   - SRH's bowling execution under dew was superior; pace bowlers (Unadkat, Mavi) kept batters under pressure

4. **Batting order vulnerability:**
   - Rinku Singh (predicted finisher, 25–45 runs) did not deliver
   - Green, Raghuvanshi fell early in chase
   - Prediction expected Rinku to bat in death overs; actual collapse suggests he didn't reach death

**Next Match Priorities:**
- Monitor Harshit Rana recovery; if unavailable, consider Kartik Tyagi for pace options
- Revisit Varun's role; may need rotation or confidence rebuild
- Review chase batting; early wicket scenarios more likely than first predicted

## Game 022 Post-Match Analysis (KKR vs CSK, April 14, 2026)

   - Noor Ahmad 3/21 in 4 overs destroyed KKR's middle order at Chepauk
   - Raghuvanshi (who showed 168 SR in recent games) collapsed vs Noor specifically
   - **Critical pattern:** KKR's RHB-heavy middle order (Raghuvanshi, Rinku, Green) is susceptible to quality left-arm wrist-spin on turning pitches

## Team Style

### Batting approach
Significant change from 2024. Loss of Andre Russell removes the greatest
T20 finisher weapon. Cameron Green at 4–5 provides batting + bowling.
Rinku moves up. Narine remains unpredictable — can destroy or fail
cheaply. Less explosive than KKR 2024 but potentially more consistent.

### Bowling approach
Pathirana's slingy yorkers + Mustafizur's cutters = distinctive death
identity. Varun Chakravarthy is the primary spin threat — devastating
at Eden Gardens. Narine still bowls 4 economical overs. Green provides
seam-bowling AR option.

---

## Strengths

- **Spin at Eden Gardens:** Narine + Varun is among the strongest spin
  pairings in IPL history.
- **Death bowling:** Pathirana + Mustafizur is an excellent combination.
- **Cameron Green's all-round value:** Batting at 4–5 and bowling 3–4
  overs gives KKR balance.

## Vulnerabilities

- **No Russell replacement:** Massive hole in death-over finishing.
- **Pace depth:** With Harshit Rana out, seam options beyond Pathirana
  and Mustafizur are thin.
- **Rahane's T20 strike rate:** ~120–125 career may be too slow for
  a modern IPL opener.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Eden Gardens suits spin-heavy approach. Dew assists first-innings
bowling from Narine and Varun. Larger outfield rewards fielding and
running. Low-scoring spin battles where Varun is match-winning.
