## Team Style

### Batting approach
Built around Jaiswal — one of the most talented young batters in world
cricket. Explosive opener who can dominate powerplays. Parag at 3 is
aggressive and capable. Hetmyer is a proven death-over finisher.
The addition of Jadeja at 5 adds batting depth and left-handed variety.
Sam Curran provides useful batting lower down.

### Bowling approach
Archer + Farooqi is an outstanding new-ball pairing — raw pace and
left-arm swing. Bishnoi provides leg-spin with accuracy. Jadeja bowls
4 overs of left-arm spin. Curran offers left-arm seam. Deep bowling
options give RR flexibility.

---

## Strengths

- **Pace bowling:** Archer (150 kph+) and Farooqi (left-arm, swing +
  pace) is one of the best opening bowling combos in the IPL.
- **Jadeja + Curran trade acquisitions:** Transform RR's all-round
  balance. Jadeja provides spin, batting, and fielding. Curran adds
  seam, batting, and death-over reliability.
- **Jaiswal:** Potential tournament-best batter. Capable of winning
  games single-handedly.
- **Bowling depth:** 7 bowling options if Jadeja + Curran bowl.

## Vulnerabilities

- **Middle-order reliance on overseas:** Hetmyer must fire for the
  middle order to function. If he fails, Parag and Jadeja carry heavy
  responsibility.
- **Split home venues:** Jaipur + Guwahati means adapting to two
  different surfaces and travelling more.
- **Parag as captain:** Young captain (21–22). Tactical maturity
  under pressure is unproven at this level.
- **Archer's fitness:** Historic injury concerns. If Archer breaks
  down, the bowling attack loses its spearhead.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Jaipur's dry conditions suit Bishnoi's leg-spin and Jadeja's left-arm
spin. Archer can be effective anywhere. Guwahati is relatively unknown —
lower prediction confidence for RR matches there.
