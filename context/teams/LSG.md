## Team Style

### Batting approach
Pant opens aggressively — left-handed, unorthodox, capable of
destroying any bowling attack in the powerplay. Pooran provides
explosive middle-order power. Marsh adds stability and power at 4–5.
Badoni is a talented young Indian batter developing rapidly. The
lineup has match-winners but can be inconsistent.

### Bowling approach
The pace battery is potentially the most fearsome in IPL 2026.
Shami provides swing and seam at 140 kph. Nortje bowls at 150+ kph.
Mayank Yadav is India's express pace sensation (155+ kph). Avesh Khan
adds depth. Hasaranga provides leg-spin with a strong googly. This
is a genuinely scary bowling attack — if Shami, Nortje, and Mayank
are all fit and firing simultaneously.

---

## Strengths

- **Pace bowling depth:** Shami + Nortje + Mayank Yadav is arguably
  the fastest bowling combination in IPL history. All three can bowl
  145–155+ kph consistently.
- **Rishabh Pant:** Match-winner. Left-handed aggression in the
  powerplay is devastating. Also an excellent keeper.
- **Hasaranga's all-round value:** Quality leg-spinner + useful lower-
  order batting.
- **Overseas depth:** Multiple quality overseas options gives tactical
  flexibility.

## Vulnerabilities

- **Shami's fitness:** Post-surgery return. Workload management is
  critical. If Shami breaks down, the attack loses its leader.
- **Mayank Yadav's durability:** Injury-prone. Has never played a
  full IPL season. High-reward but high-risk selection.
- **Indian batting depth:** Beyond Pant and Badoni, the Indian batting
  options (Abdul Samad, Himmat Singh) are unproven.
- **Too many overseas options:** Selecting the right 4 from 7 quality
  overseas players every match is a selection challenge.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Lucknow's variable pitch is a mixed blessing. On pace-friendly surfaces,
Shami/Nortje/Mayank are devastating. On slow/low pitches, the express
pace is less effective. Hasaranga's spin is useful if the pitch turns.
LSG need to assess the Lucknow surface match-by-match.
