## Team Style

### Batting approach
KL Rahul anchors. Miller provides left-handed finishing. Stubbs is
explosive middle-order talent. Depth exists but reliant on Rahul
building platform — if he falls early, middle order faces new-ball
pressure. Axar bats usefully at 6.

### Bowling approach
Starc: express left-arm pace + swing in powerplay. Kuldeep: elite
wrist-spinner, constant wicket threat in middle overs. Axar: economical
left-arm spin. Ngidi/Jamieson add pace depth.

---

## Strengths

- **Kuldeep Yadav:** Elite wrist-spinner on all surfaces. Middle-over
  wicket-taking is a major asset.
- **Starc's new-ball threat:** 150 kph left-arm pace + swing.
- **Overseas batting depth:** Stubbs, Miller, Duckett — multiple
  power-hitting options.

## Vulnerabilities

- **KL Rahul's strike rate:** Historically criticised for slow T20
  batting. SR 115 in the powerplay puts DC behind.
- **Indian pace depth:** Without Starc/Ngidi, reliance on Mukesh Kumar
  and Natarajan — decent, not elite.
- **Captaincy unproven:** Axar as IPL captain is relatively new.

---

## Home Venue Fit

Delhi's variable surfaces test versatility. Flat tracks suit the
batting depth. Slower surfaces suit Kuldeep + Axar in middle overs.
Delhi heat affects workload management for Starc.
